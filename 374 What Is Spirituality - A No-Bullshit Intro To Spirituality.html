What Is Spirituality - A No-Bullshit Intro To Spirituality

  • Understanding Spirituality: In this section, Leo Gura from Actualized.org begins by acknowledging his previous bias against the word spirituality, discarding it as something irrational and not deserving of serious study. However, through his personal development journey, he came to understand and appreciate what spirituality truly embodies.
  • Spirituality vs. Metaphysics: He proposes that spirituality and metaphysics are in fact synonymous and identical in nature. Gura defines metaphysics, and by extension spirituality, as an investigation into the fundamental nature of existence - an exploration that treads deeper than surface-level scientific investigations.
  • Metaphysics Misconception: He addresses a common notion among scientifically-minded individuals who tend to dismiss metaphysics as abstract, unprovable speculations. Gura counters this perspective on the basis that such a dismissal without actual inquiry is in itself unscientific and nonempirical.
  • Better Understanding of Metaphysics and Spiritualty: Gura encourages viewers to understand that spirituality is about developing a deep metaphysical connection with reality, stating that this involves an elevated consciousness  an experience of awe and wonder  about the profundity and beauty of existence. He gives concrete examples, such as watching a beautiful sunset, observing stars, examining a cell through a microscope, and appreciating intricate physics formulas or exquisite art.
  • Eliminating Negative Connotations: Gura takes a moment to differentiate spirituality from religion, emphasizing that although the two terms often get interchanged, they are not the same, and that spirituality does not encompass the delusions and falsehood found within religious practices.
  • Atheism and Spirituality: Being an atheist himself at one point, Gura admits to his aversion towards spirituality. However, he realized that being atheistic or scientifically-minded should not prevent one from connecting deeply with the phenomenon of existence and experiencing the awe that comes with it.
  • Spirituality  Not Just Emotions and Feelings: Gura underlines that spirituality goes beyond feelings and emotions, and involves discovering the beauty and wonder in all aspects of reality. He encourages viewers to personally explore spirituality to fully comprehend and appreciate its depth.
  • Spirituality and Emotional Connection: In this part of the video, Leo Gura explains that people can feel a deep, metaphysical connection to reality through various experiences like playing a immersive video game, watching an epic film, marveling at space exploration progress, or working in a field like science, where the pursuit of understanding connects an individual with a profound sense of reality. However, this connection is often dismissed or underestimated, especially by strategic individuals who perceive such feelings as mere emotions unrelated to objective truth.
  • Amplifying the Connection: Gura also adds that these emotional connections only represent a tiny portion of spirituality, and that true spirituality involves amplifying this connection so that it's being felt continuously. This requires not relying on specific external stimuli or events but developing a perpetual consciousness of the profundity of existence, which is often neglected in everyday life due to distractions or mundane routines.
  • Spirituality vs Rationality: Gura further addresses the dismissive attitudes of rationalists and scientifically-minded individuals who insist on hard facts and consider feelings and the sense of wonder as unimportant to the understanding of reality. But he argues that their lives are primarily governed by feelings, including their pursuit of science and rationality. Spirituality is about removing current beliefs and fantasies about reality and becoming more conscious of the true nature of existence, leading to a sense of profundity and awe.
  • The Essence of Spirituality: Lastly, Leo asserts that spirituality isn't about adopting fantastical ideas, but it's about deconstructing existing beliefs and misconceptions about reality until the raw truth remains. This inquiry and discovery process can lead to profound insights and a deeper connection to reality, making one realize the significance and beauty of existence beyond their ordinary perceptions. Gura argues that genuine philosophical inquiry and spirituality are pretty much identical concepts aimed at exploring the nature of existence.
  • Understanding Spirituality and Metaphysics: Leo explains that the aim of philosophy is the search for absolute truth and the understanding of reality, the essence and substance of everything, and the concepts of time and space. Leo emphasizes that this isn't about just harboring beliefs but about questioning and removing current beliefs to get closer to the truth. Several great philosophers, many of them idealists, have undertaken this quest and have arrived at the absolute truth.
  • Misconceptions about Accessing Truth: Many, including well-known philosophers like Kant, believe that absolute truth isn't directly accessible but can only be reached through formulas, models, or other symbolic representations. Leo counters this view by claiming that the truth is directly accessible through serious effort and spiritual practice.
  • Defining Spirituality: Spirituality is described as developing a metaphysical connection with reality, seeking the absolute truth independently of all belief systems. It's about having a personal union with the truth, "having sex with reality." Committing one's life to seeking absolute truth is seen as the ultimate spiritual act.
  • Alternative Definitions for Spirituality: Leo provides several alternative definitions for spirituality. It's about developing a metaphysical connection with reality, seeking answers about the nature of existence, truth-seeking with a capital 'T', and experiencing a personal union with the truth.

Just as one cannot fully understand an orgasm without experiencing it, one cannot comprehend spirituality without personal exploration and experience.

These challenges arise from the fact that unlike universally understood experiences like a sexual orgasm, spiritual experiences often require personal effort and are not experienced by all. As a result, explaining the profound nature of these experiences can be difficult.
