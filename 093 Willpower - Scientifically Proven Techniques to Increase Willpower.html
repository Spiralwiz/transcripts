Willpower - Scientifically Proven Techniques to Increase Willpower

  • Definition of Willpower: Willpower is the ability to control one's own impulses, which include getting out of bed, resisting unhealthy food, getting to the gym, and starting a project.
  • Importance of Willpower: Willpower is crucial for success and pursuing an extraordinary life. It helps overcome fear, advance in careers, stay on track with life purpose, and maintain healthy relationships. It allows individuals to live in integrity with values and follow through on vital activities.
  • Willpower as a Muscle: Willpower, which is equated with the prefrontal cortex muscle, can be developed. Just as muscles atrophy from disuse, the brain, specifically the prefrontal cortex, also atrophies without use. Each time one resists a lower urge, it builds up discipline and willpower.
  • Exercising Willpower: Like a physical muscle, building up willpower takes consistent exercise over time. Each resisted temptation strengthens willpower. Conversely, failure to resist certain urges results in a loss of willpower.
  • Building Willpower: Meditating and exercising are scientifically proven ways to build willpower. Meditation, a practice thousands of years old, re-sculpts the prefrontal cortex, while exercise requires discipline, strengthening willpower. Deep, slow breathing also aids willpower by increasing awareness and allowing better impulse control. Incorporating these practices consistently is key to building and maintaining strong willpower.
  • Deep slow breathing for willpower: Deep, slow breathing exercises can enhance willpower by inducing calmness and enhancing awareness. They can be practiced in conjunction with meditation or separately. It's advised to make sure one's exhale lasts longer than the inhale for maximum calming effect and to encourage connection with our bodies and thoughts.
  • Taking right action to build willpower: Engaging in disciplined behaviours or right actions can boost willpower. These actions can include waking up on time, sticking to healthy eating habits, working devotedly, and consistently following meditation and gym routines. Significant enhancements in self control and energy levels can be witnessed by sticking to these patterns for just a week.
  • Potential destroyers of willpower: Certain habits and behaviours can significantly drain willpower, including excessive television watching, internet browsing, snacking and emotional eating, irregular sleep schedules, alcohol, drug and tobacco consumption, video gaming, and pornography usage. Cutting these out can prevent the depletion of willpower reserves and energy, and reduce guilt associated with engaging in unhealthy behaviours.
  • Action steps for improving willpower: To enhance willpower, it's recommended to identify one positive behaviour (e.g., meditation, exercise, regulated sleep pattern) to incorporate into our routine, and one destructive habit to eliminate. Consistent pursuit of this dual strategy can significantly elevate willpower and overall life satisfaction.
