What Should I Do With My Life - How To Find Your Passion For Life

  • Importance of Finding Life Purpose: Leo Gura advocates for finding a life purpose that is deeply personal and valuable to the individual. The purpose should be worthwhile, needing substantial effort and dedication. Leo notes this is a challenging, lengthy process, filled with doubt and obstacles.
  • The Role of Imagination and Visualization: Emphasizing on the need for imagination, Gura urges viewers to do visualization exercises to develop a vision for their life. This vision should be based on what would excite and motivate them, making them want to strive harder and break out of their comfort zones.
  • The Effect of Fear and Doubt: According to Leo, fear, particularly fear of the unknown, often prevents people from developing ambitious visions and dreams. It's crucial to set aside this fear while laying out dreams and goals, to allow for an expansive vision of life.
  • Barriers to Achieving Dreams: Gura lists both external (e.g., residing in a less developed country, debt, obligations, health issues) and internal (e.g., self-doubt, previous traumas, perceived inadequacies) factors as barriers that can limit dream fulfillment. Despite these challenges, he emphasizes the necessity of pushing boundaries and thinking beyond current realities.
  • Need to Think Bigger and Believe in Possibility: Gura encourages viewers to think bigger and to have faith that achieving their dreams is possible, irrespective of their past experiences or their present situations. He warns against letting oneself become restricted by past or current circumstances, and advises maintaining a focus on future potential.
  • Need to buy into one's dreams: People often feel stuck or unmotivated because their psychology does not allow them to believe in their dreams. Those who attempt to envision their dreams limit themselves, fearing that their aspirations may be unattainable.
  • Importance of overcoming self-imposed limitations: It's crucial to overcome self-imposed limitations which can inhibit individuals from vocalizing or even acknowledging their big dreams. To battle this mindset, one can exercise jumping into the scary, intimidating vision and embrace faith to gain value from the exercise.
  • Taking time to craft a compelling vision: Finding one's purpose is not an overnight process. It requires individuals to disconnect from everything and engage with the question of their most compelling future vision. This vision should reflect long-term aspirations and not just immediate desires.
  • Purpose includes personal and societal impact: In visualizing the future, the individual should bear in mind aspects of personal desires such as relationships and fitness but also consider the kind of impact or contribution they want to make in the world.
  • Thinking big and embracing potential: Individuals often limit their visions under the belief that they are being too unrealistic. It's beneficial to think big in terms of contributions one can make in the world. This can involve creating something, improving a system, or making meaningful industry innovations.
  • The role of motivation in achieving visions: Without motivation, action towards achieving big visions might not occur. Hence, defining a substantial vision that stirs up enough motivation is a good starting point. The vision might not be fully realized, but working towards it can provide practical ideas for making a significant impact.
  • Taking action towards the vision: Regardless of the scope of one's vision, it is necessary to take massive action towards realizing that vision. To initiate this process, one must disconnect from distractions and reflect deeply on what they really want their life to be. Such reflection is very crucial to discovering what they should be doing with their life.
