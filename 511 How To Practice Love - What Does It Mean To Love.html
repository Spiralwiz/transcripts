How To Practice Love - What Does It Mean To Love

  • Understanding the Nature of Love: Leo Gura discusses the profound metaphysics and practical aspects of love, acknowledging that understanding and embodying love remains a struggle. Consciousness is identified as the fundamentally underlying nature of love and neglecting love can lead to physical and psychological dysfunction.
  • Practical Ways to Express Love: The video talks about showing love to another person through actions. This could mean giving attention and awareness, maintaining physical proximity, building connection, and taking care of their needs. It implies recognizing and valuing the existence of the loved one.
  • Importance of Love in Survival: Leo further highlights research citing the essential role of love in the survival and healthy psychological development of infant monkeys, rats, and humans. He suggests contemplating why consciousness needs love to function optimally and suggests viewers to practice expressing love in their day-to-day interactions.
  • Expression of Love through Actions: Leo demonstrates how to express love on a day-to-day basis through a list of actions. These include giving attention, spending time, maintaining physical closeness, forming connections, offering care, survival support, encouragement, acceptance, understanding, collaboration, and validation. He emphasizes the need to practice these actions to truly understand what it means to love someone.
  • You are Consciousness: The lecture emphasizes that we are consciousness within a larger consciousness, which is the universe. He suggests people should treat consciousness as the explorer, and the larger reality as the thing to connect deeply with so as to understand love better in practical terms.
  • Importance of Physical Proximity in Expressing Love: Physical contact, holding, touching, hugging are identified as fundamental ways to express love. They create a physical connection which is an essential aspect of love. Even simple physical proximity can evoke feelings of love.
  • Connection at Different Levels: Love involves forming a connection at different levels - physical, emotional, intellectual, and spiritual; the spiritual connection being the most profound. Emphasizing connecting in every way possible, Leo notes that real love is about complete connection.
  • Taking Care of Loved Ones: Another practical way to express love is through taking care of the person. This implies helping to meet their needs and ensuring their survival. Leo explains that when one genuinely loves someone, they appreciate the existence of the person and would support their continued existence.
  • Potential Attachment Formed through Love: The act of expressing and receiving love could lead to attachment, creating a bias because one becomes invested in the survival and well-being of the loved one. This might present a challenge, as the loved one is a finite being, and their existence could potentially be threatened.
  • Incorporating others into our sense of self: Love involves extending personal sense of self to incorporate others, making their survival and experiences tied to our own. This opens up individuals to vulnerability as harm to loved ones is perceived as harm to self, but enhances depth of love.
  • Limitations of focusing love: Focusing love on specific entities in consciousness limits its potential. Attaining deeper and universal love involves transcending attachments, including the instinct to help people survive.
  • Protection of loved ones: Protecting loved ones from suffering and trauma is integral to love. Parents, for instance, gauge how much suffering and challenge a child can take, providing a protective shield without over shielding to allow room for personal growth.
  • Taking on a loved one's agenda: An aspect of love is taking on the agenda of a loved one and considering it an extension of personal agenda. Incorrectly imposing our agenda on loved ones is a common mistake.
  • Reciprocity within love and care: Loving individuals should treat others in the manner they would want to be treated. Genuine joy in the success of loved ones indicates the existence of a deep connection.
  • Acknowledging differences: Love includes respect for the individual's specific wants and needs. It involves championing the highest aspirations of others, even when it differs from personal aspirations.
  • Importance of loyalty: Loyalty, such as having someone's back, forms a crucial element of love. Lack of faith in a friend or partner's loyalty weakens the sense of love and trust.
  • Respecting the individuality of others: Leo Gura stresses the importance of respecting the individuality of others in a love relationship. This means acknowledging their unique needs, passions, and visions, and supporting and encouraging them in these, without trying to impose one's own desires or expectations on them. This also involves viewing them as equals and not trying to manipulate, control, or dominate them.
  • The value of sacrifice in love: Gura discusses the value of sacrifice in a love relationship, explaining that if you truly love someone, you would be willing to work for them and make sacrifices on their behalf. He mentions the negative attitudes existing in certain communities and the need for mutual willingness to sacrifice in a balanced relationship.
  • True acceptance and appreciation: For genuine love to exist, Gura indicates that there must be total acceptance of the other person as they are, without judgment or a desire for them to change. In this context, love involves appreciating the individual for their own sake and seeing their intrinsic beauty.
  • The concept of 'sovereignty' in love: A significant facet of love involves respecting the 'sovereignty' of the other person. This means acknowledging their individual consciousness and treating them neither as superior nor inferior, but as equals. This fundamental recognition prevents manipulation, control or exploitation.
  • Encouragement aligned with the other's interests: Gura elaborates on how encouragement in love should align with the interests and passions of the loved one, especially in the context of self-exploration, self-expression, and self-actualization. Encouragement should not be based on oneÂ’s own priorities or expectations but rather support the passions of the loved one.
  • Significance of sacrifice in love: In the context of love, Gura emphasizes the significance of sacrificing for the benefit of the loved one. He points out that being willing to work and make sacrifices for the one you love is essential in a genuine love relationship. This is applicable for both in their unique ways and based on the needs of each other.
  • Presence of beauty in all consciousness: Gura asserts that everything is beautiful as all forms of consciousness hold intrinsic beauty. This requires a deeper connection and willingness to appreciate the existence of the object or individual for its own sake.
  • Appreciating Things Deeply: Love is about intensely appreciating the thing you love. Just like how one would appreciate every detail, every brush stroke of a Van Gogh painting, love lies in appreciating the tiny details of another person.
  • Beyond Needs: In love, the other person is not a tool to satisfy your own needs. Real love lies in not needing anything from the other and appreciating their existence.
  • Respecting Other's Perspective: Love also means respecting the other person's point of view. When you love someone, you want to understand their perspective, even if it varies from yours. This is an essential component of love as every individual has unique experiences and perspectives.
  • Embracing Diverse Points of View: Being open-minded is key to deeply loving someone. You have to be mentally flexible to accommodate the varied points of view, appreciate the diversity of their experiences, and not impose your perspective onto them.
  • A Deep Understanding: Love is about understanding the deep subjective experiences, consciousness and spirit of the other person. This understanding goes beyond their physical body and works to deeply connect and bond with their mind and spirit.
  • Validating the Other: One important aspect of love is validating the other person's point of view and showing them respect. Caring about their interests and sharing similar interests builds a strong connection and is the basis of love.
  • Being There for Others: Love tests the depth of a relationship during times of hardship. Being there for the other person when they are at their lowest, showing them support, and helping them not to feel lonely are signs of true love.
  • Validating Feelings: Love also requires validating the feelings of the other person and empathizing with them. A powerful way to build an emotional connection is to frequently ask them how they are feeling and validate their responses.
  • Connection and Collaboration: Love fosters a sense of togetherness, companionship, and collaboration. It's about being there for each other, working together, and providing support, especially during challenging times.
  • Importance of Emotional Connection: Emotional connection in love can be as important as physical, intellectual, or spiritual connection. For a fruitful and genuine relationship, a deep understanding of the other's emotions is crucial. This understanding is achieved by actively listening and empathizing with them when they share their feelings.
  • A Deeper Path to Love: The ability to handle diverse points of view, appreciate differences, and empathize with the emotions and experiences of others, lead to a deeper understanding of love, and can help strengthen relationships.
