Contemplation - The Most Important Tool For Sages

  • Definition of contemplation: Contemplation is openly pondering a question with focus and without bias to inquire into the nature or truth of that question. It involves setting aside time, selecting a question of genuine interest, pondering it from "ground zero", and maintaining focus on that subject for a minimum of five to 10 uninterrupted minutes.
  • Sage's key contemplative question: The most significant question a sage can ask to understand the essence of reality is, "What is it?" This question can replace "it" with any object or thing the contemplator wants to inquire about.
  • Range of topics for contemplation: Contemplation can be applied to a wide variety of domain - from personal life areas like career and relationships to existential questions about the nature of reality, philosophical, scientific, or artistic questions. The list of themes to consider is vast, ranging from society, life, color, love, anger, desire, science, mathematics, culture, freewill, spirituality, truth, economy, education, passion, atheism, intuition to information and more.
  • Impact of contemplation: Contemplation can be transformative as it allows individuals to delve deeper and gain insights into varied aspects of life and existence. It encourages individuals to question and investigate matters they usually take for granted.
  • Contemplation vs. Fantasy: It is important to distinguish real contemplation from fantasy or hearsay. True contemplation begins from ground zero, without preconceived notions, hopes, or biases, and not based on what one has read or been told.
  • Spiritual and mystical aspect of contemplation: The more profound aspects of contemplation may tap into spiritual and mystical states of awareness.
