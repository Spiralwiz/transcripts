Satisfaction Meditation - How To Make Meditation Enjoyable!

  • Importance of satisfaction meditation: Leo Gura introduces a new meditation technique which he calls 'satisfaction meditation'. This practice aims to help individuals regain control of their happiness by not relying on external validation or stimulation. Instead, the focus is on just being in the present moment and finding satisfaction in it. This technique not only takes back control of the happiness mechanism which often relies on external rewards, but also moves away from constant chasing, problems and fears that rob one of natural joy.
  • Practicing satisfaction meditation: The meditation practice requires setting aside at least 10 minutes, and involves simply sitting with open eyes in a comfortable position without trying to control, manipulate or stop thoughts, or achieve a particular enlightened state. What is essential is nudging oneself into being satisfied with the present moment. This can create a positive feedback loop where being aware of the satisfaction can lead to more satisfaction, essentially becoming satisfied with the satisfaction. It is also important to resist the mind's tendency to wander off to problems, desires or fantasies about better scenarios and focus on being satisfied with the existing circumstance.
  • Effects of consistent practice: With consistent practice, this simple satisfaction can deepen and potentially lead to pleasure, bliss and love for existence itself. By bringing this practice into everyday life, one can start to experience deep satisfaction in simply existing, which in turn can peak into ecstatic joy. However, the aim should not be to seek these ecstatic experiences or to improve existence but to simply be mindful in the moment.
  • Challenges with satisfaction meditation: Leo acknowledges the challenges with this practice. One's mind is trained to constantly chase better experiences or improvements, which can hinder the satisfaction with simply being. Other distractions like fantasies about better scenarios, sex, drugs, social gatherings can lead to dissatisfaction as it detracts from the satisfaction of the present moment. To avoid this, it is advised to not want anything other than the present moment and to stop chasing experiences.
  • Greater implications of satisfaction meditation: Over time, practicing satisfaction meditation can lead to finding joy and happiness in simple existence and can help one fall in love with consciousness and existence. Furthermore, through consistent practice, this satisfaction can develop into deeper feelings of joy, bliss and love for existence itself. Therefore, it can be beneficial to make this practice a regular part of daily life. It is stressed that seeking high states of spiritual consciousness or bliss should not be the primary goal.
  • Importance of being present in meditation: Leo Gura discusses how the desire to reach future states of mind, such as enlightenment or mystical experiences during meditation, often leads to unhappiness as these states are not met. He emphasizes the importance of being present and satisfied during meditation, rather than using it as a means to an end.
  • True happiness and satisfaction: The video highlights finding contentment in the present moment and simply accepting existence as it is. This forms the basis of true happiness, as opposed to the constant pursuit of future pleasures.
  • Loneliness and people pleasing: Both circumstances stem from dissatisfaction and a perception that the present moment lacks something, leading to addictive behaviors and feelings of emptiness. Transforming the perception through the satisfaction meditation eliminates feelings of inadequacy and neediness and can alleviate feelings of loneliness.
  • Practical applications of satisfaction meditation: Leo Gura suggests implementing satisfaction meditation in everyday situations, from waiting in a queue to enjoying alone time. This practice enhances the ability to stay content in challenging situations and alleviates feelings of dissatisfaction and annoyance.
  • Caution against overreliance: He cautions against misusing the technique to avoid social interactions and create isolation. Satisfaction meditation is not a shield against the world but a tool for deriving joy and satisfaction out of personal interactions and increasing non-neediness.
  • Misusing satisfaction meditation: Leo urges viewers not to misuse the satisfaction meditation technique as a means to achieve enlightenment or mystical experiences. Chasing such experiences can prevent the practitioner from being fully present and content with their current state of existence.
  • Overcoming obstacles in meditation: Meditation routines can often seem like a chore or a mechanical exercise when the practitioner doesn't enjoy the process and views it as a means to an end. Leo suggests letting go of future-oriented goals and focusing on the satisfaction derived from simply being in the present moment.
  • Importance of Satisfaction Meditation: This meditation method focuses on gaining happiness in the present moment rather than chasing future happiness or experiences such as enlightenment. It requires practice to counter years of survival-oriented conditioning and can lead to a subtle shift where meditation becomes enjoyable rather than a chore.
  • Cultivation of Satisfaction: This practice does not produce quick, sharp peaks of pleasure like dopamine hits but creates a steady, lasting satisfaction. Practicing satisfaction meditation regularly can spill over into other areas of life, potentially leading to deep joy, bliss, and love from the present moment.
  • Dopamine Circuit vs. Satisfaction Circuit: The dopamine circuit in our brain creates short-lived spikes of joy, like finding money, while the satisfaction circuit yields sustained, even-keeled satisfaction. Dopamine hits, as experienced in activities like playing video games, are temporary and addictive whereas contentment from the satisfaction circuit is steady and can be amplified through practice.
  • Ineffectiveness of Chasing Dopamine Peaks: Activities providing dopamine hits like earning millions, winning jackpots, drugs, fancy food, cars, etc., all provide a temporary high but ultimately lead to dissatisfaction due to their unsustainability. Satisfaction meditation, not based on dopamine, contributes to a lasting and sustainable sense of contentment.
  • The Misunderstanding and Dismissal of Satisfaction Meditation: Owing to its simplicity, satisfaction meditation is often dismissed or overlooked by individuals who prioritize tangible results over inner contentment. However, it targets building an enduring ability to be satisfied with mere existence, a skill most people lack.
  • Role of Satisfaction in the Pursuit of Happiness: Practicing satisfaction meditation can serve as a root solution to the happiness problem many people face. Despite emphasizing satisfaction, this does not negate the importance of maintaining a strong work ethic in other areas of life like business and finance. It does, however, teach to detach one's happiness from these external pursuits.
  • Importance of Finding Satisfaction in Moment: Leo Gura emphasizes that the key to lasting satisfaction is continuous appreciation of the present moment and regular practice of his satisfaction meditation. If these habits are not cultivated, people may still find themselves unhappy even in the most idyllic settings.
  • Problems Caused by Inability to Find Satisfaction: Gura points out that this inability to find satisfaction under perfect conditions can lead to profound unhappiness, often pushing people into depression or worse. He argues that trying to arrange our existence to please us is a misleading notion and the real path to satisfaction involves training ourselves to be content with existence as it is.
  • Long-Term Benefits of Satisfaction Meditation: Gura discusses that consistent practice of satisfaction meditation will lead to a greater sense of contentment and peace, eventually leading to profound states like mystical experiences and awakenings. He suggests that these states are more likely to be reached when people genuinely enjoy their meditation practice.
  • Enjoyment of the Journey: Gura further expands this idea to the processes of self-actualization, personal development, and work. He advocates for finding joy in these processes rather than seeing them as a grind to achieve an end result. This shift in attitude towards work, personal development, or any chosen area of life can result in a state of flow, making tasks seem effortless.
  • Balance Between Speed and Enjoyment: Gura shares a personal example of rushing through his work and personal development efforts, which resulted in achieving quite a bit but it was neither sustainable nor enjoyable. He advises slowing down and taking the time to enjoy these processes leading to a more productive long-term outcome. He suggests this might involve changing one's attitude towards work or personal life, and make the journey enjoyable rather than focusing solely on the end goal. He insists that this shift brings us into the state of flow, causing tasks to feel effortless.
  • Misplaced Focus on Quantity Over Quality: Gura criticizes the tendency to focus on achieving numerical targets rather than genuine learning and enjoyment out of life. He encourages balancing striving for self-improvement with self-acceptance and enjoyment of the journey.
  • Importance of Enjoyment Over Productivity: Leo shares a personal experience from his early personal development days about his pursuit of speed reading as a means to consume more information more quickly. He tried to practice speed reading exercises every day, but eventually realized it was only making his reading experience a grind rather than enjoyable.
  • The Downfall of Quantification: Leo cautions against quantifying everything in life, such as how many books you've read or dollars in your bank account, as these numerical metrics do not lead to true happiness or satisfaction. Instead, he encourages focusing on the enjoyment and contentment derived from these activities rather than the amounts.
  • Understanding Happiness: Leo stresses the importance of understanding happiness personally and directly through lived experiences. Differentiating between authentic happiness and temporary pleasure derived from dopamine spikes is crucial according to Leo. Authentic happiness does not result in a post-experience drop in satisfaction.
  • Approach to Personal Development: He underlines the need for taking ownership of personal development by transforming high-level abstract ideas into practical techniques and implementing them. Leo insists that the key to making the most out of his videos and personal development lessons is not passive listening but active brainstorming and implementation.
  • Finding One's Life Purpose: Finding your life purpose and aligning your life with it may not trigger instantaneous dopamine hits but will stir the satisfaction reward circuit, leading to a profound sense of contentment according to Leo. At actualized.org, resources and courses are available to assist with this.
  • Moving Beyond Abstract Ideas: Leo emphasizes mastering the skill of converting abstract high-level ideas into concrete, practical techniques for personal development. He advises brainstorming various methods and experimenting with what works best to fully embody and practice the insights derived from his videos.
  • Do not Expect to be Spoon-fed: Leo makes it clear that his purpose is not to spoon-feed practical methods to all abstract ideas he talks about but to provide insights and high-level ideas that viewers must then put into practice independently. The process of personal development requires individuals to stand on their own two feet, not rely entirely on external guidance.
  • Appreciating the Process of Reading: As an example of how applying the above principles can enhance satisfaction, Leo shares his own change of perspective on reading. Instead of focusing on the number of books read, he now takes a slower, more enjoyable pace, embracing the process rather than just aiming to finish as many books as possible.
