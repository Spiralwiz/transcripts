Understanding The Exquisite Balance Of Life

  • Understanding Balance from an Existential and Spiritual Perspective: Leo Gura presents the concept of balance from an existential and spiritual perspective, using terms like "exquisite" and "sublime" to describe this balance. He defines "exquisite" as flawless craftsmanship and subtle understanding, and "sublime" as lofty, grand, and inspiring awe. Balance is seen as a fundamental feature of reality and becomes more noticeable as one becomes more conscious. This balance is demonstrating the workings of infinite intelligence.
  • Balance, Science, and Consciousness: Scientists appreciate the balance in nature and physics, but dont fully understand its profound nature or the underlying intelligence maintaining it. As consciousness increases, individuals begin to mirror the universes balance in their own lives, thereby becoming instruments of balance in the world. Choices made by individuals contribute to maintaining or destabilizing the universe's balance, making the refinement of consciousness a force for rebalancing the world.
  • Balance as an Expression of Wisdom and Intelligence: Wisdom is described as knowing the proper balance in life and the world. Proponents suggest aligning one's life and choices with the same exquisite intelligence in nature, embodying what they term as "God's intelligence." The fool, in contrast, lacks balance and fails to see the sublime balance around them. Wisdom comes from intelligence and consciousness, and the highest wisdom is selflessness and love.
  • Dangers of Imbalance: The danger in imbalance comes from extreme desires, whether for money, power, sex, or other excesses, leading to self-destruction. The fool doesnt see far enough ahead to recognize when too much of a good thing becomes too much. To become wise involves learning to moderate ones desires, essentially preventing oneself from turning into a self-destructive cancer.
  • Beauty and Balance: There's innate beauty in balance, seen in nature's intricacy and precise workings. Balance doesn't always mean perfect symmetry or equality, but has a much more sophisticated manifestation. For example, the exquisite balancing acts in nature, represented by the precise flight of a hummingbird or intricate workings of molecular biology, express a profound beauty and balance: the result of an underlying intelligence. Such subtle observations can evoke awe and appreciation in the observer.s
