Visualization - A Powerful Technique For Reprogramming Your Subconscious Mind

  • Understanding Visualization: Visualization is a robust technique that helps to program the subconscious mind with goals and ambitions. Enhancing our self-development, it allows us to direct our subconscious towards crafting the life we aspire to live. This process is achieved through detailed and vivid mental imagery that programs our current goals into our subconscious mind, which in turn motivates us to take action towards achieving them.
  • Effect of Visualization on Self-Image: Self-image is a worldview that essentially resides in our subconscious mind. It is composed of our beliefs about ourselves, the world, and how it operates. Visualization plays a crucial role in reshaping this self-image influencing our beliefs about our strengths, weaknesses, and capabilities.
  • Process and Importance of Visualization: The process of visualization involves clearing our mind, focusing on our goals, vividly imagining them and the positive emotions associated with them. Emphasizing the importance of consistency, it is recommended that visualization be practiced daily and action be taken towards achieving these visualized outcomes. By doing so, our lives can progressively align with the envisioned image.

  • Basic steps in Visualization: The fourth step of visualization involves deep and calm breathing for one to two minutes with closed eyes. This step isn't mandatory but helps to create a more powerful visualization by calming and centering the self in the present moment.
  • Vivid Imagery: The visualization process then continues with setting a timer for five to ten minutes where one should vividly visualize their goals. Use specific examples rather than abstact ideas, and fill the mind with detailed and vivid imagery. Enhance the visualization by involving multiple senses such as sound, touch, smell, and taste which makes it feel real and impactful.
  • Visualization in the Present Moment: While visualizing, it's important to imagine the goal as if it is already achieved in the present moment rather than thinking of it as a future event. This makes the mind more accepting of the imagery and intensifies the emotional experience.
  • Repetition and Consistency: To effectively reprogram the subconscious mind, visualization should be done daily with consistency. Skipping days or weeks can hinder the process. Devise a routine to do visualization like clockwork every day at the same time, treating it as a top priority task.
  • Overcoming Resistance: Resistance from the subconscious mind can be experienced during visualization, especially because the mind can rebel against the present moment visualization if the current reality greatly contrasts the desired goal. In such cases, intensify the visualization efforts to push through the resistance and train the subconscious mind.
  • Meditation Practice: Visualization is likened to a meditation practice where longer and consistent practice leads to more benefits. Initially, it may take 2-4 weeks to start seeing the results, but continued practice can lead to more transformative results.
