The Power Of Asking Questions

  • The Importance of Questioning: Leo stresses that the quality of one's life highly depends on the questions one asks. He claims that most life problems exist due to the lack of right questions and emphasizes questioning as a simple and neglected technique that leads to personal growth.
  • Freedom Through Questioning: He asserts that answers to life questions are found within ourselves, not externally. Despite emphasizing the importance of introspection, Leo does not dismiss studying and learning from external sources. He views it as raw data that can further provoke internal reflection and individual understanding.
  • Google as an Information Source: Google is represented as a tool that can transform one's life if utilized correctly. Leo emphasizes asking high-quality questions for filtering down the overwhelming amount of data available.
  • Questioning as an Ongoing Virtue: He reinforces that questioning is an aspect applicable to both beginners and advanced individuals. Constant improvement and elaboration are encouraged as this technique can be refined and mastered over the years.
  • Questioning in Everyday Life: Contrary to his previous episodes focusing more on existential contemplation, this episode focuses on ordinary and practical questions pertaining to daily life.
  • Examples of Questions: Several examples are listed, such as "What do I really want?", and "What is the purpose of my life?". Leo urges his viewers to understand that these questions are supposed to be asked repeatedly and continuously throughout life, thus refining one's understanding and evolving their answers.
  • Quality Over Quantity: He emphasizes the idea that quality questions matter more than the quantity. He suggests that these questions act as powerful keys that unlock profound understanding and insights.
  • Advice on Crafting Custom Paths: Leo highlights the significance of crafting customized paths for personal and spiritual development. By incorporating individual strengths, weaknesses, cultural backgrounds, abilities, and interests, these custom paths could lead to a successful and fulfilled life.
  • Questioning as a Custom: He intimates that quality questions are a cornerstone to one's life and that we could guide or define our life course based on the questions we ask.
  • Recognizing Responsibility: Leo Gura encourages asking oneself questions about personal responsibility, such as "how am I failing to take responsibility for my life?" This prompts introspection and encourages personal growth and self-improvement.
  • Understanding Consciousness: Gura suggests asking "what does it mean to be more conscious?" to understand the true essence of consciousness through personal experience. To better comprehend abstract concepts often discussed in spiritual and philosophical talks, he suggests asking for concrete examples.
  • Assessing Worldview: Gura advises asking "how is my world view holding me back?" and "what is my world view?" This encourages individuals to explicitly define and understand their worldview and evaluate how this might be impeding their progress.
  • Exploring Interests and Dislikes: To understand personal interests and dislikes, Gura suggests asking "why do I love X?" This creates a deeper understanding of personal values and sources of inspiration and demotivation.
  • Planning Professional Growth: Gura recommends contemplating "what is the next stage of evolution for my business or for my career?" to develop forward-thinking strategies and follow a progressive trajectory in professional life.
  • Clarifying Personal Desires: Gura stresses the importance of asking "what do I truly believe that I need to have to be happy?", "why do I meditate?", and "why do I want this relationship?" These questions can help individuals clarify their desires and understand what drives their actions.
  • Asking High-Quality Questions: Gura emphasizes the power of high-quality questions in changing perspectives and initiating fresh thought processes, which can lead to new insights and breakthroughs.
  • Turning Inwards for Success: Gura suggests asking "how do I become successful?" and turning inward for answers. He also acknowledges the importance of learning from external resources and effective individuals around.
  • Understanding Uniqueness: Gura proposes questions like "how am I unique?" and "how am I not being true to my uniqueness?" to help individuals discover their unique qualities and determine how they might be failing to live authentically.
  • Personal Development and Self-reflection: Gura recommends reflecting on one's best and worst qualities by asking "what am I like when I'm at my best?" and "what am I like when I'm at my worst?" Such introspection can aid personal development and growth.
  • Facing Fears and Resistances: Gura advises inquiring "why am I resisting X?" or "why am I afraid of X?" to understand fears and resistances that may be hindering personal advancement.
  • Visualizing Ideal Job and Regrets: Leo suggests considering "what would be my ideal job?" and "what will I regret most on my deathbed?" These questions can provide valuable insights into career aspirations and life decisions.
  • The power of questioning oneself: Leo Gura emphasizes asking insightful questions to make sense of various situations in life, especially when faced with failures, problems, or the completion of a project. When an endeavor concludes, asking "what's the key lesson here?" can lead to valuable insights that may have otherwise gone unnoticed.
  • Gaining wisdom from books: After reading any book, asking the question "what are the top five ideas that I learned from this book?" and jotting it down can help extract the most important lessons and remember them in the future.
  • Learning from negative examples: Negative individuals or scenarios can teach valuable lessons, provided one stops judging and honestly seeks to understand them. An example mentioned is how one can learn about the negative consequences of egotism, unconsciousness, and selfishness from public figures with such qualities.
  • Identifying root problems and finding permanent solutions: Often, people solve problems without addressing their roots. Taking time to genuinely inquire about the root cause of problems can help recognize and address the heart of the issue. Similarly, asking the question "what would a permanent fix for this be?" can help find longer-lasting solutions.
  • Improving authenticity and perspective: Questions like "what would be a more authentic way of doing this?" or "how does this look from another person's point of view?" can offer fresh perspectives and lead to meaningful changes in how situations are perceived and addressed.
  • Identifying triggers, opportunities, and assumptions: Questions like "why does this trigger me?", "what opportunities am I overlooking?", or "what if my core assumptions are wrong about this situation?" can lead to personal growth, better opportunities, and improved perceptions.
  • Using questions to generate solutions and ideas: Questions such as "what's a new way of seeing this problem?" and "who could give me good advice on this topic?" can open up new possibilities and help identify potential solutions or avenues for advice.
  • Gaining insight into oneself: Questions like "what am I really trying to say here?" and "how can I make this task more enjoyable?" can provide personal insight and improve one's worldview or daily tasks.
  • The effect of persistent questioning: While the answers to serious and profound questions may sometimes take weeks, months, or even years to appear, the results are usually rewarding and life-transforming. His favorite types of questions are the ones that bring about long-term answers and profound changes.
  • The Process of Asking Questions Can Take Time: It's normal for answers to take weeks, months, or even years to emerge, but having a genuine curiosity and belief in the questions are pivotal in finding the answers. Sometimes, avoidance of certain topics can delay the process. The questions that seem the most difficult or intimidating often lead to the most profound personal transformations.
  • Ideological Beliefs Can Impede Questioning: People who hold rigid ideological or dogmatic views may struggle to formulate and entertain meaningful questions as they are often invested in maintaining and defending certain preconceived notions.
  • Belief in Questions and Answers is Important: To effectively ask deep, transformative questions, there needs to be a belief not only in the questions, but also in the significance of their answers. This belief will allow for dogged persistence, even when answers arenÂ’t immediately apparent.
  • Genuine Curiosity is Crucial: Authentic questioning comes from a place of genuine curiosity, not a need for validation or rationalization of a personal situation. Questions framed negatively or with a pre-determined bias can block authentic inquiry and the receipt of truthful answers.
  • Silent Periods Nourish Thoughtful Inquiry: To effectively ponder deep questions, time must be set aside free from outside distractions. This requirement often contrasts with societal norms that encourage constant engagement with work, socialization, and entertainment.
  • Phrasing and Framing Questions Matter: How a question is framed impacts the nature and quality of the responses. Succinct, specific, bias-free phrasing can lead to more incisive and true insights.
  • Open-Mindedness and Receptivity are Key: The answers to deep questions might often be unexpected, uncomfortable, paradoxical, or counterintuitive, requiring an open mind and readiness to engage with all potential responses.
  • Trust Your Intuition and Authority: The subconscious mind can produce answers that are more accurate and more aligned with personal needs and experiences than those obtained from external sources.
  • Understanding Reality as Understandable: Reality is perceivable and understandable. Even if a question initially seems impossible to answer, the discovery of incorrect assumptions or poor framing within the question is in itself a valuable revelation.
  • There's No Such Thing as Impossible Questions: All questions, no matter how challenging they seem, can potentially be answered. This belief allows for the pursuit of even the most difficult existential queries.
  • The Power of Persistent Questioning: In life, Leo encourages individuals to train themselves to ask questions every day, in all situations for the rest of their lives. He asserts that this technique is applicable in almost all situations and for all problems. One's answers tend to come from completely unexpected sources, either from their own inner voice, intuition or subconscious mind, as well as from the outer world, from magazines, conversations with people or even unanticipated moments like being in a shower.
  • Enhanced Perception through Regular Questioning: By regularly asking questions, one primes their mind to be receptive to the answers which could come from any source, including the world around them. This primes our selective mind to be receptive to what we are searching for, causing our mind to connect the dots and answer the questions we have been asking. He terms this as serendipity and synchronicity which can only be activated through consistent questioning.
  • Achieving a Continuous Stream of Insights: Persistent and habitual questioning cultivates one's mind to constantly receive answers and insights, leading to improved confidence and resourcefulness in dealing with any arising problem. Developing this occurs by years of questioning and receiving high-quality answers, which then makes one feel as if the universe is their personal Google that answers everything subsequent to their questioning.
  • Proposed Action Steps to Enhance Questioning:
  • 1. Contemplating the importance of questioning.
  • 2. Starting a list of top-life questions.
  • 3. Determining which questions one genuinely cares about.
  • 4. Identifying chronic problems and brainstorming high-quality questions related to these issues.
  • 5. Generating a list of questions one is afraid to ask.
  • 6. Buying a stack of legal pads and pens and leaving them around the house to jot down new questions regularly.
  • The Importance of Outside-the-box Questioning in Different Fields: Artists, entrepreneurs, scientists, spiritual practitioners and psychonauts need to ask deeper, broader, and more outside-the-box questions to excel in their fields. This holds for every occupation and even for life-transforming psychedelic experiences.
  • Fundamental Questions Leo Asked: Some of Leo's life-changing questions include understanding what consciousness is, what reality is and what he genuinely wants. These, according to him, have taken years, even decades to answer but have been incredibly rewarding.
  • Understanding as a Priority: Leo puts a high importance on understanding, stating it as the foundation for everything else, including awakening, mastery and embodiment of awakening. Accurate, profound understanding leads to all of life opening up, and is viewed as not just a means to an end, but the ultimate end itself. It is the purpose for life and the key to achieving it is asking quality questions.
