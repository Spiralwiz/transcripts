Life Coaching - The Powerful Benefits Of Working With A Life Coach

  • Understanding life coaching: Life coaching is a discipline aimed at improving specific areas of life where one is facing challenges. It involves obtaining third-party perspectives, accountability, new insights, and strategies from a coach to overcome psychological issues and improve life quality.
  • Difference between therapy and life coaching: Therapy addresses severe dysfunctions like depression, anxiety, and mental disorders to restore normalcy. Meanwhile, life coaching takes individuals from a functional level to an optimal level, focusing on improving life's quality.
  • The logistics of coaching: Life coaching is usually conducted once a week over the phone due to impracticalities and inconveniences of in-person sessions. The phone calls typically last between 15 to 30 minutes.
  • Coaching process: In a coaching session, the coach inquires about the client's problem or challenge. They ask questions to help the client gain clarity and address their issues, offering new perspectives and breaking down mental barriers. Coaches also hold the client accountable for their actions and commitments.
  • Importance of a coaching relationship: A well-functioning coaching relationship involves commitment from both the coach and the client. Paying for coaching services denotes seriousness and dedication towards personal improvement.
  • Ultimate goal of coaching: The ultimate goal of life coaching is to push an individual beyond their normal limits, to achieve a high-quality, optimum level of life. This is achieved by squeezing out their maximum potential, pushing them to constantly aim higher, and holding them accountable for their actions and goals.
  • Coaching is a proactive exercise: The client takes initiative and presents their problem or situation, and then the coach and client collaboratively work through it to find a solution.
  • Coaching refocuses on client's thought process and building action plans: The aim of coaching is to help the client reshape how they see the world, their beliefs, and psychology. Simultaneously, the coach assists in delineating an action plan that the client can execute.
  • Assignments are vital in coaching cycle: Each coaching session concludes with certain assignments or action items for the client, such as journaling, visualization exercises, affirmations, or actions tied to the specific changes they want to make in their life.
  • Accountability through follow-ups: The coach does follow-up in subsequent meetings to discuss progress and challenges, and to assign new action items.
  • Power of coaching: While the full power of coaching might be challenging to quantify, it can lead to subtle & profound shifts in client's life, changing their perspective about relationships, business and can help in overcoming limiting beliefs.
  • Misunderstandings about coaching: It's a common misbelief that coaching is only for people with disturbed lives. In contrast, successful people continually work on improving their lives, contributing to their overall success.
  • Coaching is about recognizing potential: Coaches help clients identify and exploit their unrealized potential, pushing them to the next level. While these enhancements may not be immediately visible, they can make a substantial difference over the long term.
  • Coaches benefit from having coaches: Even coaches can gain value from being coached as it provides a fresh perspective and pushes them to reach their potential, very much like how they guide their clients.
  • Coaching is about being honest and genuine: A good coach will know intimate details about the client's life, their strengths and weaknesses, and will provide straightforward feedback, aiding in personal development and improvement.
  • Coaching reduces the learning curve in personal development: Coaches provide strategies and consultations, help to overcome challenges, and accelerate the learning process from years to just a few months. They serve as a beneficial resource, directing you to the right information, ideas, and insights needed for breakthroughs in your life.
  • Proactivity in coaching relationship: Success in coaching requires proactivity from the individuals being coached. They must present their challenges and issues as well as ask vital questions to get the necessary answers.
  • Coaching and successful people: High-performing individuals like CEOs and professional athletes frequently employ coaches. This is because coaches can provide unique insights, inspire, identify potential, and offer different perspectives that may not have been considered, enhancing their professional performance.
  • Coaching for clarity: Often, people may already know the answers to their problems but lack the ability to articulate them. Coaches help provide clarity on life's direction, goals, strengths, weaknesses, and areas to work on, thereby, significantly reducing the learning curve.
  • Investing in coaching despite cost considerations: While some may consider the cost of coaching a hindrance, Leo argues that the time saved and progress achieved far outweigh the initial expense. Coaching isn't always a lifelong commitment; profound realizations, insights, and strategies can be obtained in a few months. These can be applied throughout life, justifying the cost.
  • Objections to coaching due to cost as a limiting belief: Leo suggests that if an individual needs coaching but is deterred due to cost, this is a limiting belief that holds back personal growth and development. Such individuals are encouraged to invest in coaching for their general life improvement.
