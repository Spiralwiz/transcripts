The Top Dangers Of Using Psychedelics

  • Overdosing on psychedelics: Overdosing on psychedelics can occur in mild or extreme forms. Mild overdosing may lead to negative experiences or physical harm, while extreme cases can potentially lead to death. It's vital to understand the correct dosages for each substance and to remain well within these limits. People often overdose due to negligence in measuring their dosages or by underestimating the power of psychedelics.
  • Experiencing physical harm: The use of psychedelics can lead to self-inflicted physical harm, especially when users lose control over their movements or find themselves in dangerous environments. To avoid these situations, individuals should discipline themselves to sit still and consume psychedelics in safe indoor environments where there's a low risk of physical injury.
  • Risk of vomiting: Users tend to vomit on psychedelics, especially when they consume the substance right after eating. To avoid this discomfort, individuals should wait at least four hours after eating before taking psychedelics.
  • Bad trips: Tripping on psychedelics can result in negative experiences, often referred to as 'bad trips.' These are different for everyone, but can involve experiencing the worst nightmares or fears in vivid detail. This can be especially terrifying during a psychedelic peak when one's sense of time is distorted, causing the experience to seem like it's lasting much longer than it actually is.
  • Inexperience with psychedelics: Inexperienced users are at a higher risk of experiencing negative side-effects from psychedelics. Users should thoroughly educate themselves about psychedelic substances, accurate dosages, potential effects, and risks before deciding to consume these substances. A common problem is individuals taking a dose that is too high, often due to underestimating the strength of psychedelics or attempting to gain bravado or bragging rights.
  • Risk of ego inflation: There's a danger associated with ego inflation when users consume high doses of psychedelics in a competitive or boastful manner. Experiences with psychedelics should be about gaining consciousness rather than simply reaching higher doses.
  • Influence on personal lifestyle: The use of psychedelics can have a significant impact on a person's lifestyle, potentially leading to reckless behaviors or inconsiderate decisions. Users should remain aware of the consequences of their actions in the physical world, even while under the influence of psychedelics.
  • First-time users: First-time users are more likely to have negative experiences as they may underestimate the strength of psychedelics. It is essential for them to do their research about the substances, use accurate dosages and prepare themselves mentally for the trip. Proper guidance from more experienced users can also be beneficial.
  • The importance of spacing psychedelic trips: For optimum understanding and spiritual growth, it is important to space out psychedelic trips over time. Overuse or frequent consumption of psychedelics can lead to negative effects, instead of the intended self-development.
  • The impact of psychedelics on perceptions of reality: Psychedelics can provide experiences and perceptions that are far removed from regular reality. Understanding and accepting this aspect is essential when considering the use of these substances.
  • Positive take on psychedelics: Despite the various dangers, psychedelics can also provide access to experiences otherwise unattainable, such as exploring complex concepts like God, love, and consciousness. However, this potential benefit needs to be weighed responsibly against the potential dangers.
  • Advocacy for safer usage of psychedelics: Advocates of psychedelics, like Leo Gura, aim to promote safer use of these substances. This involves raising awareness about dangers, advocating adherence to safety protocols, and encouraging responsible usage.
  • Continuous learning from psychedelic use: Psychedelic use should be an ongoing learning process where individuals continuously learn from their experiences, increase their understanding, and implement safety measures based on previous experiences.
  • Horrific Trips lasting for Eternity: When in a bad trip with psychedelics, one may get trapped in a seemingly eternal nightmare, losing all memories and sense of their old reality. This usually occurs when dosing is too high.
  • Depressive and Negative Minds: Individuals with depressive or negative tendencies should be cautious as they are more likely to experience negative spirals during trips. The recommended measure is to lower doses to avoid bad trips.
  • Facing Fear and Trauma: Psychedelics deconstruct one's mind and reality, forcing out repressed trauma. Dealing with these traumas is necessary for personal growth but must be gradually faced with low to moderate doses to prevent overwhelming experiences.
  • Psychological Instability and Uprooting of Reality: High psychedelic doses can result in psychological instability, existential crisis, and even psychosis. It can lead to depression, a feeling of insanity, and loss of joy for life, hence it needs to be managed delicately.
  • Potential for Confusion: Psychedelics can invoke confusion about reality and life. The stress from this confusion can last for an extended period and negatively impact coping mechanisms.
  • Mental Illness Complications: Persons with mental illnesses need to be extra cautious with psychedelics as they can exacerbate mental health symptoms. Severe cases should avoid them altogether, while mild cases may still benefit from responsibly administered low to moderate doses.
  • Personal Development Work: Psychedelics are beneficial but should not be solely relied upon for personal development or spiritual growth.
  • Use of Psychedelics with Mental Illnesses: Psychedelics may aggravate or help certain mental illnesses depending on their severity and the substance used. Individuals with mental illnesses should research the effects and experiences of others with the same condition before using psychedelics. For some, particularly those with untreatable mental illnesses, psychedelics could potentially aid in their treatment. There are existing clinical treatments and upcoming FDA-approved treatments using psychedelics for depression and PTSD.
  • Interference with Pursuit of Success: Psychedelics can derail the momentum of pursuits such as business, dating, and family life. They make material accomplishments seem trivial and meaningless, potentially leading to a lack of motivation in these areas. For instance, users might start to question their career or family obligations. This questioning can be beneficial in some cases, but detrimental in others depending on individual circumstances.
  • Escape from Basic Life Challenges: Psychedelics may be used as an escape from tackling basic life challenges, such as building a career, managing emotions, and maintaining relationships. Regardless of the spiritual or mystical experiences from psychedelics, individuals still need to face and develop success in the material reality. It can be detrimental to neglect the material aspects of life.
  • Isolation Due to Psychedelic use: Extensive use of psychedelics could lead to withdrawal from friends and family, thereby promoting an anti-social mindset. There is a danger in becoming too isolated upon becoming heavily involved in psychedelics.
  • Misuse of Psychedelics: Misuse or abuse of psychedelics can have negative effects such as decreased motivation and productivity, questioning important components of life, avoiding tackling basic life challenges, and becoming too isolated. The choice to use psychedelics should be made responsibly and with a full understanding of their potential effects. Precautions should be taken to mitigate the risks.
  • Incorporating Psychedelics for Spiritual Growth: Psychedelics can provide an easier and faster way to access spiritual truths than through meditation or retreats. However, they can lead to misconceptions about enlightenment and misunderstandings about reality if not used responsibly. While controversial, there is potential for psychedelics to be used for spiritual growth and human development if a responsible path to doing so is established.
  • Risk of Neglecting Material Life: While psychedelics can provide extraordinary experiences and insights, it is important not to neglect the material aspects of life, as this can negatively impact spiritual pursuits. A balance between the mystical and material domain is crucial for overall wellbeing.
  • Integration with Daily Practices: Despite the powerful and immediate effects of psychedelics, it is essential to maintain daily practices like meditation and self-inquiry for a steady growth in consciousness and personal development.
  • Misinterpretation and Misuse of Psychedelics: With the high potential for misinterpretation and misuse of psychedelics, it is crucial to approach them with caution and a clear understanding of their effects. Psychedelics should not be relied upon as the sole path for personal growth and understanding, but rather carefully incorporated into a broader self-improvement plan.
  • Access to Higher Realms: Psychedelics can provide access to higher realms of consciousness, such as love, trauma healing, and self-understanding. Depending on usage, they can have either positive or negative outcomes. Despite their potential, it is important to carefully consider the legal implications of using psychedelics.
  • Legal Implications: The legality of substances should be researched thoroughly to understand the potential consequences of their use. Supplying others with psychedelics can pose legal risks and could lead to charges of drug dealing. It is crucial to understand the legal system, potential sentences, and to take precautions to avoid reckless or illegal activities.
  • Medicinal Interactions with Psychedelics: Certain medications can have harmful interactions with psychedelics. It is essential to be aware of potential lethal interactions and to stick to one chemical at a time to understand its effects and avoid confusion.
  • Risks of Chronic Use: Chronic use of psychedelics can erode your mind, sense of reality, and ability to think straight. To avoid the risk of dependency or addiction, the frequency of psychedelic use should be moderated and spaced out over time.
  • Danger of Misinterpreting Mystical Visions: Misinterpretation of mystical visions can lead to false notions of enlightenment, a messiah complex, and other delusional behaviors. To benefit from mystical experiences, one must have a clear, unbiased mind so as not to corrupt the psychedelic experience with personal needs and desires.
  • Impact on Productivity and Financial Success: Psychedelic use can have a significant impact on productivity and material success. Psychedelics can decrease motivation for material productivity, leading to financial losses. This trade-off should be carefully considered before delving into psychedelic experiences.
  • Legal Risks: Engaging in the extraction, possession, or supply of certain substances can invite legal risks, often leading to lengthy prison sentences. Additionally, online scams are common when purchasing psychedelics from unreliable sources. Driving under the influence of psychedelics can lead to impaired judgement, accidents, and subsequent legal repercussions.
  • Risk of Using Psychedelics in Relationships: Psychedelics can also influence relationships, potentially leading to questioning and potential problems within those relationships. Users should be mindful of maintaining a balance in their lives and responsibly managing their relationships while using psychedelics.
  • Isolation due to extensive psychedelic use: Continuous and frequent use of psychedelics can lead an individual to become isolated and uncommunicative, causing withdrawal symptoms and leading to an antisocial and misanthropic behavior. To prevent this, balancing the use of psychedelics with a regular social life is advised.
  • Making rash decisions post-trip: After a profound psychedelic experience, individuals might make life-altering decisions impulsively which they might regret later. It is recommended to avoid any serious decisions for at least a week post-trip until completely stabilized.
  • Demotivation in meditation practice: Psychedelics can take individuals to a high level of consciousness that might seem unachievable through regular meditation practices, which can demotivate people in their meditation routines. However, it is important to maintain a routine spiritual practice for a steady growth in baseline consciousness.
  • Falling into chronic usage: Excess usage of psychedelics can erode the mind and one's sense of reality. Using these substances more than once in two weeks is not advisable, as it gives insufficient time to understand and integrate the insights gained from each experience.
  • Misinterpreting mystical visions due to ego: Post-trip, individuals might misinterpret mystical visions and insights due to the presence of the ego, leading to pitfalls like false notions of enlightenment, the creation of a messiah complex, and the corrupt interpretation of mystical truths. Recognizing and being aware of the ego's self-deceptive nature can prevent these corruptions.
  • Misinterpretation of Psychedelic Experiences: Psychedelics can exacerbate biases and self-deceptions of individuals. This is why it is important to have a self-inquired, ego-less, and pure mind. Failing to do so can lead to incorrectly interpreting these experiences, mixing them with the individual's personal self-deceptions and egotisms.
  • Partial Understanding of Reality: A single or few psychedelic trips might lead someone to think that they've understood the entire truth. In reality, they've only experienced a small facet of it. Getting the whole picture typically requires several trips over a period of time.
  • Disconnection from God and Psychedelic Addiction: Using psychedelics may lead to a feeling of connection with God or feeling high levels of love. Once this wears off, individuals might face the pain of feeling disconnected from God, which might lead to a psychological addiction to the substance.
  • Effect on Quality of Life: Despite providing the most profound experiences, psychedelics can lead to a decrease in the quality of life off the drug. This can lead to psychedelic use becoming an escape from reality and obligations.
  • Potential Trigger for Suicidal Thoughts: Psychedelics can potentially trigger suicidal thoughts for various reasons like derealization, incomplete awakening, or a want to merge with God. However, for some, they may also serve as an cure, showing them something beyond their physical existence. It is recommended to immediately discontinue usage if suicidal thoughts emerge.
  • Legal Risks: Psychedelics are illegal in many parts of the world. Using, possessing, or supplying them can lead to significant legal repercussions. It is important that users familiarize themselves with the specific legality of substances in their area and consider the potential consequences of usage.
  • Understanding legal consequences of psychedelic use: Users should be aware of the legal repercussions of psychedelic use such as potential prison sentences. Countries, states, and cities vary in their laws regarding psychotropic substances. Researching and understanding such laws is critical.
  • Avoiding the use of unknown substances: It is hazardous to ingest unknown substances. Users should have a clear understanding of the substance they are using, including its source and accurate dosage information.
  • Avoiding impure substances: Substances from unreliable sources can contain significant impurities, which increases the risks associated with psychedelic use. To avoid this, users should take substances from trusted sources.
  • Avoiding substances that cause physical harm: Certain psychedelics like ketamine, MDMA, nBOMes, DXM, and nitrous oxide can cause physical damage, especially when misused. Classic psychedelics such as DMT, mescaline, mushrooms, and LSD are generally safer for the body.
  • Avoiding dangerous substances: Salvia and deterra are regarded as harmful and should be avoided due to the chaotic experiences they can induce.
  • Being cautious with certain medications: Interaction between psychedelics and certain medications, especially SSRI antidepressants, can be lethal. It is essential to conduct thorough research on potential interactions if on any medication.
  • Avoiding mixing substances: Combining two or more chemical substances can lead to unexpected and potentially fatal outcomes. It is safer to stick to one chemical at a time to understand its effects without confusion.
  • Being wary of recreational drug use: There is the risk that use of psychedelics could lead to experimentation with recreational hard drugs like cocaine, heroin, meth, and crack. A clear distinction between recreational drugs and psychedelics should be made.
  • Avoiding chronic marijuana use: Frequent marijuana use, especially when combined with psychedelic use, can lead to negative outcomes, including addiction.
  • Avoiding unsafe environments for psychedelic use: Using psychedelics in crowded areas, parties, raves, outdoor settings, etc., can lead to harmful situations. Comfortable and private spaces are recommended for use.
  • Being cautious with research chemicals: Many research chemicals are relatively untested and can lead to unpredictable or harmful effects. Always verify the substance and its effects before usage.
  • Being aware of Hallucinogen Persisting Perception Disorder (HPPD): HPPD can occur with heavy or prolonged psychedelic use, which might result in long-term visual and auditory distortions or other sensory disturbances.
  • Potential risks of using psychedelics: One risk is the onset of HPPD (Hallucinogen Persisting Perception Disorder), where lingering effects of psychedelics can cause visual and auditory distortions. It is unsure what causes HPPD, but it could be due to genetics or dosage, and it may interfere with an individual's ability to enjoy normal life.
  • Legal implications of dealing with psychedelics: Attempting to extract, synthesize, or deal model substances can lead to heavy legal consequences. If caught manufacturing drugs, charges can be severe, possibly resulting in lengthy prison sentences, hence taking such risks should be avoided.
  • Pitfalls of online purchase of psychedelics: Be aware of potential scams and fraudulent dealers while procuring substances online, as there is a risk of the product not being delivered or money being stolen.
  • Danger of driving while intoxicated: Once a psychedelic substance is consumed, limit movement until the effect is almost gone to avoid harm and potential accidents, especially driving.
  • Common errors while using psychedelics: Some common mistakes include treating psychedelics like toys, immaturity, consuming large doses, frequent and chronic usage of psychedelics, traveling in dangerous areas, mental instability, and a lack of theoretical foundation.
  • Misconception about the understanding of psychedelics: The belief that a complete understanding of psychedelics can be gained from a few trips is a misconception. It takes around 100 experiences to gain a substantive understanding of the potential of these substances.
  • Understanding of the risks involved in life: It is important to understand that every aspect of life carries risks, be it driving, sex, business, or relationships. Psychedelics are no different, and like in life, these risks can largely be managed by being thoughtful, mindful, and careful. Users should approach the use of psychedelics with maturity and caution.
  • The Long-Term Process of Psychedelic Use: Leo Gura emphasizes that understanding the potential of psychedelics requires a long-term commitment spread across multiple years and dozens to hundreds of trips. He urges individuals to approach this process carefully and methodically, taking into account any incurred risks, especially as the frequency of usage increases.
  • Making Personal Decisions about Psychedelic Use: According to Gura, the decision to use psychedelics is personal and not to be taken lightly. While he provides resources for understanding the dangers and potential benefits of psychedelics, he leaves the ultimate choice to the individual.
  • Combining Psychedelics with Other Techniques: Gura argues that psychedelics offer a direct and effective approach to spiritual growth and understanding reality. However, he recognizes that they should be used in conjunction with other techniques such as meditation, yoga, and self-inquiry.
  • The Importance of Psychedelics in Understanding Reality: Gura suggests that without psychedelics, some truths about reality, God, love, and consciousness may remain inaccessible to those who are not spiritually gifted. He argues that offering a direct, easy, and quick access to these realities benefits individuals, even if they do not attain permanent enlightenment. Gura stresses that psychedelics should never completely replace other spiritual practices.
  • Responsibility of Pioneering a Spiritual Path of Psychedelics: Gura aims to establish a path toward the highest metaphysical understanding and spiritual realizations using psychedelics in a responsible manner. He looks to pave a way that doesn't negate other practices but incorporates them into a spiritual journey augmented by psychedelics.
  • Changing Society's Views on Psychedelics: Gura believes in the necessity of reshaping society's perspective on psychedelics. Acknowledging the civic duty he feels, he maintains an unwavering commitment to promoting responsible psychedelic use practices. In this, he promises a zero-tolerance approach towards any misuse or exploitation.
  • Sharing Further Discoveries About Psychedelics in the Future: Gura hints at having made multiple discoveries regarding psychedelics yet to be shared. He promises future explorations into this topic, further contributing to his educational resources in the field of psychedelics.
