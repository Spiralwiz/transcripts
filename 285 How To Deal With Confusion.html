How To Deal With Confusion

  • Embracing Confusion: Confusion is a common experience when on the path of self-actualization. It can present itself in various aspects of life: discerning one's purpose, making vital life decisions, understanding concepts such as consciousness, God, and free will. Understanding must be created as the default position in life is ignorance.
  • Confusion and Mind: The mind seeks certainty and order leading many people to fear and run away from confusion. This can significantly hinder the learning process, which heavily involves understanding and reconciling opposing concepts or beliefs. So, one requires to be comfortable with confusion and consider it a lifelong process of reconciliation and learning.
  • Emotional Tolerance for Confusion: One's inability to tolerate uncertainty leads to a knee-jerk reaction to immediately solve the confusion, leading to rigid, mechanical behavior. However, one hallmark of self-actualized individuals is their tolerance for uncertainty, mystery, and paradox. Thus, developing emotional tolerance for confusion is crucial for personal growth.
  • Five-Step Process to Embrace Confusion: Leo Gura proposes a simple five-step process to identify and embrace confusion: 1. Awareness: Noticing and feeling the confusion, 2. Acknowledging: Recognizing the confusion and accepting its existence, 3. Facing: Questioning why the confusion exists leading to the realization of unresolved information or uncertainty, 4. Permitting: Allowing the state of confusion without resisting it and seeking immediate solutions, 5. Surrendering: Entering a state of not-knowing and giving up the need to know.
  • Applying the Five-Step Process: This process applies to all aspects of life. In understanding complex subjects like enlightenment, one often feels confused. Ignoring this confusion is common, however, acknowledging and embracing it can be beneficial. Applying the five-step process to such situations can lead to intellectual robustness and possibly enlightenment, contradicting the conventional belief of resolving confusion for knowledge.
  • The Art of Withholding Answers: Experienced Zen masters and non-duality teachers often refrain from providing direct answers, instead promoting confusion and surrender. This approach encourages a continuous learning and discovery process beyond a single moment of enlightenment. Avoiding moments of confusion hinders potential profound insights into life, personal growth, and progress.

  • Embracing Confusion Step-by-Step: Leo Gura describes a five-step process to embrace confusion.
  • - Step 1: Awareness: Recognizing where and how confusion manifests in your body, such as sensations in your head or chest.
  • - Step 2: Labeling the Confusion: Acknowledging and naming your state as "confusion," which most people avoid doing.
  • - Step 3: Facing the Confusion: Asking yourself what's causing the confusion and being honest about not having a resolution yet.
  • - Step 4: Allowing the Confusion: Accepting the state of confusion, challenging the resistance you may feel against it.
  • - Step 5: Entering a State of Not-Knowing: Surrendering your need to know or have immediate answers, freeing yourself up for deeper exploration.
  • Applying the Process to Enlightenment Work: In spiritual pursuits like self-inquiry or enlightenment work, confusion often arises. Instead of ignoring or pushing through the confusion, these five steps can be utilized to better understand and embrace the state of not-knowing.
  • Applying the Process to Practical Life Decisions: The process of embracing confusion can also be useful when making practical decisions like determining a career path or choosing a diet.
  • Anticipated Objection: Ignorance: Leo counters the argument that embracing confusion might lead to ignorance or stupidity. He explains that ignorance arises from ignoring what one doesn't know and possessing a false sense of certainty.
  • Importance of Revelation: Leo argues that the most profound insights and knowledge arise not from persistent pushing and trying to force answers, but through revelations. Embracing the state of not-knowing places one in the best position for such revelations.
  • Embracing Confusion for Personal Growth: If one aims for personal growth and creating an accurate model of reality, it's important to be honest about things they don't understand or have answers to. This curiosity and openness lead to more intellectually robust and grounded individuals.
  • Confusion and Decision Making: Recognize that not every answer will come immediately, and some decisions may take time to resolve organically without forcing it. This involves being comfortable with uncertainty and embracing confusion for the sake of a more authentic and profound understanding. This applies to significant decisions such as identifying a life purpose and intending for the answer to come from a divine source or inner intuition rather than the ego.
  • Impacting Life Quality by Ignoring Confusion: Need for immediate, clear answers can lead to emotional instability and decreased creativity. Constant desire for certainty solidifies rigid beliefs, leading to defensiveness and closed-mindedness. Not embracing confusion limits exploration of reality's mysteries and hinders use of intuition, which is essential for quality of life.
  • Embracing Confusion Leads to Profound Insights: Tolerating confusion for an extended period can yield profound insights. Deep exploration of various subjects requires a high tolerance for uncertainty and confusion, allowing for a broad and deep understanding of multiple topics. Resistance and lack of tolerance to confusion decides whether you persist with your research or quit prematurely.
  • Navigating Confusion in Life Purpose Selection: "Almost-paralysis" due to confusion while deciding between two life purposes is normal. It suggests that you are not embracing the confusion and trying to rush into a decision. This positioning typically leads to asking others for immediate answers rather than patiently allowing the authentic answer to reveal itself.
  • Confusion and Its Role in Life Quality: Constantly needing to know and settle the confusion can cause emotional instability, forming rigid beliefs that lead to defensiveness and a lack of open-mindedness. Ignoring and not tolerating confusion leads to closed-off insights and prevents a deep exploration of topics, hindering personal growth. On the contrary, embracing confusion and uncertainty may open the path to profound revelations about life and reality.
  • Defending the Value of Embracing Confusion over Immediate Resolution: Forced quick answers are usually low quality as compared to the profound and true insights achieved by embracing confusion. Allowing mystery and uncertainty may take longer and require more patience but ultimately leads to understanding that extends beyond one's ego.
  • Appreciating the Mystery of Reality: Suppressing the mystery of reality by not embracing confusion conduces to stifled creativity and an artificial sense of certainty about reality. Accepting confusion and exploring the mysterious aspects of reality can lead to researching new states of consciousness, new possibilities, and groundbreaking discoveries.
  • Insight Blocks Due to Reluctance to Embrace Confusion: Profound insights in life need a person to tolerate confusion for an extended period. Constant need for certainty prevents one from exploring topics deeply and insulating oneself from valuable insights and learnings. Recognizing and embracing confusion are significant steps towards personal growth and understanding life more holistically.
  • Implications of Ignoring Confusion: Ignoring confusion can lead to a solidification of beliefs, resulting in ideological conflicts and defensiveness. It also hampers intuitive understanding and limits opportunities for deep insights in life. This can lead to quitting endeavors prematurely due to a lack of tolerance for confusion, becoming an impediment to personal growth.
  • Importance of Tolerance for Confusion while Gathering Knowledge: Ability to handle confusion is helpful when intensively researching across fields and trying to correlate different pieces of information. This process becomes overwhelming without high tolerance for uncertainty and confusion. It might require time, but it results in comprehensive knowledge that can lead to life-altering insights.
  • Embracing confusion for personal growth: Leo Gura emphasizes the importance of embracing confusion instead of being stuck or quitting. By acknowledging uncertainty and moving forward regardless, people can gradually find resolutions and keep progressing in their self-actualization journey.
  • Effects of an inability to tolerate confusion: Leo Gura explains that intolerance for confusion often results in a person quitting or getting stuck easily in their decision-making or action-taking process. This advice led Leo to reevaluate his attitude towards gaining knowledge. He realized that his attempts to force and find answers instead of embracing the confusion were counterproductive, leading to a decision to surrender to the uncertainty.
  • The importance of surrender and acceptance for enlightenment: Emphasizing the ineffective nature of forcing answers for nuanced topics like enlightenment, Leo defines an ideal approach to involve surrendering to confusion and accepting uncertainty. He acknowledges that this process was challenging because of his rooted belief in intellectual power and the importance of having answers.
  • The role of Zen Masters and Non-duality teachers in fostering confusion: Leo discusses how experienced Zen Masters and Non-duality teachers often avoid giving direct answers. Instead, they encourage students to surrender to and be comfortable with confusion. He discusses the Zen tradition of Koan practice that involves working through a riddle for years, which is designed to force students to accept their confusion.
