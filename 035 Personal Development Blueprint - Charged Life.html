Personal Development Blueprint - Charged Life

  • Importance of Charged Life: The term "charged life" represents an idea to strive for an extraordinary life, pushing oneself out of the comfort zone, initiating challenging tasks such as starting a new business or engaging in social events, and effectively breaking away from a comfortable life to avoid complacency. A daily or weekly commitment to living a charged life is proposed.
  • Attributes of Charged Life: The charged life requires intentionally designing challenges. It involves consciously choosing challenges that align with personal desires and goals rather than being thrust upon by external factors or circumstances. Embracing the charge life brings satisfaction from overcoming these created challenges rather than being bombarded by unexpected burdens.
  • Rewards from Charged Life: Opting for a charged life might involve more work than a comfortable one, but it promises much higher returns in terms of personal growth, creative contribution, more love, joy, higher consciousness, achievement, a sense of accomplishment, and pursuit of excellence.
  • Characteristics of a Caged Life: A caged life is characterized by constantly reacting to environmental changes and struggles to meet basic needs, often leading to dissatisfaction and unhappiness. Such individuals often face financial struggles, conflicts in relationships and tend to indulge in negative addictions such as drugs, alcohol, and pessimistic thinking.
  • Caging of Life: Those living a caged life are in a constant cycle of negativity and pain, struggling with addictions and reactive behaviors like worrying and fostering limiting beliefs. They live with a short-sighted view of deriving immediate pleasure, which often curtails their long-term potential.
  • Breaking Away from a Caged Life: For individuals living a caged life, it's crucial to identify personal desires, break free from the cycle of negativity, and begin a transition towards a charged life. This transition is bound to provide a feeling of satisfaction and fulfillment in various aspects of life.
  • Choosing the Life: People have the freedom to choose between a caged life, comfortable life, and a charged life. Not deciding is also a decision in itself. Leo insists on consciously choosing the charged life, irrespective of the initial discomfort, as it eventually leads to a fulfilling and enriched life.
  • Living a "caged life": This is characterized by living to please other people, trying to meet others' expectations, and falling into negative addictions like overeating or negative thinking. People leading a caged life often feel stuck and overwhelmed, as if life is against them. The transition from a caged life to a charged life can be challenging but ultimately rewarding.
  • Transitioning from a "caged life": Transitioning requires identifying personal desires and working towards them. The journey involves traversing from a caged life to a comfortable life, then ultimately to a charged life. This process can be painful but rewarding.
  • Living a "charged life": This involves challenging oneself, engaging in new adventures, building new relationships, learning new skills, and improving one's health. Those who live a charged life take constant action and make a concerted effort to improve their lives every day. The rewards include higher levels of satisfaction, consciousness, tranquility, confidence, and self-esteem.
  • Recognizing the challenges of a charged life: Living a charged life isn't easy. It involves taking on new challenges, like starting a business, improving your social life, exploring new places, and learning new things. It requires dedication, commitment, and acceptance of potential failure.
  • Making a conscious choice to live a charged life: Leo Gura emphasizes the importance of consciously choosing to live a charged life instead of defaulting to a caged or comfortable life. If a choice isn't made, the individual may seemingly inevitably end up leading a caged or comfortable life. The lack of committing to a charged life could be due to unawareness of the choice or a fear of the associated challenges.
  • Seeing the "caged life" position as an advantage: Leo suggests that living in a caged life could have an advantage as it motivates individuals to break free and move towards a comfortable life and then eventually a charged life. He further mentions that many successful people have overcome difficult backgrounds, using their experiences as motivation to achieve greatness.
  • Advice for those leading a comfortable life: Leo advises those leading comfortable lives to take purposeful action and not take life for granted. He emphasizes the potential and opportunities for a fulfilling life in modern society and encourages such individuals to live a charged life.
