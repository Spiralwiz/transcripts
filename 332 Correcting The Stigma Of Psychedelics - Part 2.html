Correcting The Stigma Of Psychedelics - Part 2

  • General Stigmatization of Psychedelics: The stigma against psychedelics is not accidental but is a deliberate construct developed for controlling communities and power. Psychedelics are viewed as threatening since they question the status quo of culture, society, capitalism, and government. They challenge cultural norms and conservative values, revealing unrealized truths of reality and existence that conservatists can find perturbing.
  • Misunderstanding and Misinformation about Psychedelics: Misinformation and lack of direct experience contribute to the negative perceptions about psychedelics. There is a prevalent tendency to lump psychedelics together with hard drugs and a great deal of factually incorrect information about their associated dangers and risks. This results in further stigmatization and demonization of these substances.
  • Psychedelics vs Conservative Government Stance: Government bodies tend to follow conservative approaches, which include upholding beliefs and conservative values that sustain power at the top of societal hierarchies. Therefore, psychedelics, which threaten to disrupt these hierarchical structures, are perceived as a danger and fiercely resisted.
  • Psychedelics and Consciousness Evolution: Psychedelics have the propensity to expand consciousness and encourage selflessness, empathy, and compassion. They make individuals more conscious and question various aspects of life such as work, governance, and personal values. This potentially threatens the status quo and established ideologies.
  • Historical Persecution due to Psychedelics use: Certain societies and cultures (Aztecs, Mayans, Bwiti tribe) experienced persecution due to their use of psychedelics in spiritual practices, symbolizing the history of resistance towards psychedelics.
  • Potential of Psychedelics: Psychedelics can offer profound insights and contribute to personal growth, spiritual development, and the advancement of human civilization in various areas such as science, technology, and art. It is crucial to balance responsible use with personal growth, learning, and evolution.
  • Societal Resistance to Change and Acceptance of Psychedelics: There is a resistance towards the acceptance of psychedelics and the potential consciousness evolution it can catalyze. This is often due to societal fear towards radical change and a preference to keep current power structures intact.
  • Possible Mitigation through Psychoeducation and Regulation: To counteract the negative perceptions surrounding psychedelics, it is necessary to invest in research, loosen government restrictions, and implement education campaigns based on science and research. This could facilitate safe and responsible consumption of psychedelics and help in debunking prevalent myths.
  • Future of Psychedelics: Over the next few decades, significant advancements expected in the field of psychedelics could lead to a major societal evolution. This includes developing new therapeutic approaches and replacing current uninformed attitudes with a more enlightened perspective on psychedelics.
  • Misinformation about Psychedelics: Leo Gura addresses the misinformation on psychedelics that has been taught in schools for the past decades. This widespread indoctrination has led to generational prejudice, closed-mindedness and rigid ideas which refuse to consider scientific or clinical data that can demonstrate potential benefits of psychedelics.
  • Classification and Misunderstanding of Psychedelics: Psychedelics such as LSD, mescaline, MDMA, DMT, and psilocybin are incorrectly classified as Schedule 1 substances, implying a high potential for abuse, no accepted medical use, and lack of safe usage methods. Gura argues that these classifications do not apply to psychedelics when proper education, responsible use, and clinically proven therapeutic usage are factored in.
  • Potential Therapeutic Uses of Psychedelics: Current research and clinical trials are showing the efficacy of LSD, MDMA, psilocybin mushrooms and ibogaine in treating conditions like depression, anxiety, PTSD and drug addiction. This invalidates the claim that these substances have no clinical use or therapeutic potential.
  • Safety of Psychedelics: Contrary to the claim that there are no safe ways of using psychedelics, the psychedelic community has demonstrated responsible usage of these substances. Historical cultures have successfully integrated psychedelics into their spiritual and healing practices.
  • Comparison of Risks: Gura points out that the societal perception of risks associated with psychedelics is proportionally skewed. Far more annual deaths occur due to heart disease, smoking, alcohol, and misuse of prescription opioids than are related to psychedelics.
  • Need for Better Statistics: Gura points out the need for better statistics on psychedelic-related deaths. He emphasizes that the lack of such statistics suggests the rarity of deaths related to these substances.
  • Potential Future of Psychedelics: Gura concludes by highlighting the potential future advancements in the field of psychedelics, where sensible drug policies could lead to a more profound understanding of these substances. He believes that this could lead to societal evolution and advancement in human civilization in areas like art, science, and technology.
  • Misconceptions surrounding risks of psychedelics: Psychedelics, primarily LSD & Psilocybin, have a very low addiction and lethality potential - lower than caffeine, nicotine, alcohol, and Tylenol. Additionally, combining or mixing various substances is what usually leads to most problems with psychedelics. Leathality from these substances, such as mushrooms or LSD, generally stems from dangerous behavior while under influence rather than the substance itself causing harm.
  • Emergency Room Admissions involving drug use: The number of admissions for issues relating to the use of true psychedelics like LSD and Psilocybin is significantly lower compared to opioids, cocaine, sedatives like Xanax, marijuana, and even Tylenol. Despite the counter-argument that more popular usage of psychedelics could lead to higher admissions, both legalization and proper education about responsible use and dosage could keep these numbers lower.
  • Comparison of Psychedelics with harder substances: MDMA (Ecstasy), unlike true psychedelics like LSD or Psilocybin, does pose significant lethality risks when impure or mixed with other substances like alcohol. However, these risks could be substantially diminished with legalization, leading to better assurance of substance purity, and proper education about dosage and safe usage.
  • Perceived harm due to substance abuse: Research from the UK presents an interesting comparison between the harm caused by various substances to society and to individuals. Surprisingly, alcohol tops the list while "true psychedelics" like LSD, Mushrooms, and even MDMA are at the bottom, suggesting significantly lower harm to both society and individuals.
  • Psychedelics challenging the status quo: Psychedelics challenge established belief systems, often leading to a more open-minded perspective, which partly explains the resistance and stigma associated with their use. While they do come with their own set of dangers, risks associated with misuse, overdose, and misrepresentation of street drugs could be reduced with sensible polices, education, and establishing a proper cultural context.
  • Future prospects with psychedelics: Invested research could open new directions in the field of psychedelics and hopefully change the perception around their usage. Implementation of high-quality education programs can enable the responsible use of these substances while enhanced government funding for research can broaden the understanding of their potential therapeutic use.
  • Balancing change and progress: While embracing change can be frightening, it's necessary for progress and evolution. Psychedelics offer a methodical and objective way to explore spirituality and could become a commonplace tool for accessing profound truths and inspiring personal growth in the future. Resistance to change can only lead to suffering and standing against the cultural evolution towards higher consciousness is futile.
  • Religion vs Reality: The suppression of esoteric elements of religion through more exoteric materialistic aspects is compared to the current societal disconnect from existential truths causing issues such as addiction, mental health problems, violence etc. Legalization and proper research into psychedelics could help combating these issues as they carry significantly lesser risks compared to other legal substances.
  • Change in societal norms: Psychedelics usage is predicted to become a societal norm in the future, with future generations perceiving our current, often erroneous, attitudes towards them as backward and ignorant.
  • Misconceptions about Psychedelics: The common misconceptions about Psychedelics include that they are addictive, dangerous, without medical uses, cause psychosis, brain damage, are a 'new-age fad', 'fun and recreational', and a slippery slope to heroin use. These myths are extensively debunked by research and data.
  • Addictive Properties of Psychedelics: Psychedelics are typically non-addictive substances. They have been observed to be useful in the treatment of addiction for substances such as heroin, alcohol, and crack. They are considered significantly less addictive than substances such as caffeine.
  • Danger and Medical Uses of Psychedelics: While Psychedelics carry potential risks when wrongly used or abused, the overall danger level is significantly lower than many legal substances such as alcohol and tobacco. In addition, there are ongoing studies to realize the medical benefits of psychedelics including the treatment for mental health disorders.
  • Psychosis and Brain Damage from Psychedelics: The assumption that Psychedelics cause psychosis or brain damage lacks scientific support. However, individuals with a prior history of severe neurosis, psychosis, or mental instability should avoid the use of psychedelics.
  • Psychedelics as a 'New-Age Fad': Psychedelics have been used historically in various cultures for spiritual enlightenment and healing, indicating that they are not merely a recent trend, but part of long-standing traditions.
  • Psychedelics as 'Fun and Recreational': While some substances that fall under the bracket of psychedelics can be used for fun and recreation, true psychedelics such as LSD are often used for deeper, more profound experiences rather than just recreational purposes.
  • Psychedelics Leading to Heroin Use: There is no evidence to suggest that the use of Psychedelics is a 'slippery slope' leading to the use of more dangerous and addictive substances such as heroin.
  • Public Perception and Stigma: Despite the scientific data showing the reduced risk and potential benefits of psychedelics, they are generally perceived in society as highly dangerous, largely due to stigma and misinformation. This discrepancy between their actual and perceived dangers creates a culture of fear and resistance towards Psychedelics. The societal bias needs to be addressed to promote better understanding and responsible use of psychedelics.
  • Adverse Effects of Wrong Use: Lack of education, absence of proper cultural context, misuse, and the unavailability of high-quality substances contribute to potential dangers and risks associated with Psychedelics. Sensible drug policies and comprehensive education can mitigate these risks.
  • Better Understanding and Responsible Use: Psychedelics should not be demonized and swept under the rug. Involving scientists, clinicians, and therapists can enhance understanding and promote responsible use. They open up a methodical and objective way to explore spirituality and inspire personal growth, which makes it important to combat negative propaganda and encourage an informed approach to their use.
  • Misconceptions surrounding Psychedelics: Psychedelics are thought to be addictive, simply induce hallucinations, and are used by detached, unserious individuals. These assumptions are untrue as psychedelics have shown to inspire responsibility and awareness, encourage profound realization and learning beyond everyday reality and are used by serious thinkers across various fields for personal and spiritual development.
  • Psychedelics and Reality: Psychedelics allow a reevaluation of perceived reality, challenging personal beliefs and the status quo. They inspire open-mindedness and a questioning approach, unsettling those with solidified beliefs about life and the world. Joining the discourse about psychedelics is often misunderstood due to the culture's focus on consumerism and detached from existential matters.
  • Social Stigma against Psychedelics: Psychedelics are stigmatized as they pose a threat to established cultural, scientific, religious, political, and business norms, all intent on preserving their status quo. Psychedelics provoke questioning and reconsideration of these norms leading to a discomforting shift for many who hold these beliefs.
  • Psychedelics and Political Orientations: Psychedelics tend to shift individuals towards more liberal viewpoints. As they remove boundaries and promote a holistic view of life and global considerations, this disrupts conservative perspectives built on rigid boundaries and localized concerns. This transformation doesn't imply acceptance of all liberal beliefs but a movement towards more selfless and compassionate positions.
  • Individual and Societal Tensions: Personal and societal tensions exist as individuals and societies juggle between their established believes (ego) and the revelation of truth. Psychedelics showcase this struggle vividly.
  • Consciousness Stages and Psychedelics: The spiral dynamics model positions an individual's journey to higher consciousness in stages. These stages transition from traditional, success-oriented, hippie to higher consciousness stages involving a more global perspective. As this transition accelerates, individuals find psychedelics as a potent tool in dissolving the established barriers between these stages.
  • Cultural Evolution and Psychedelics: Psychedelics can help individuals evolve through stages of consciousness, a process modeled by spiral dynamics. This progression is often resisted both individually and collectively, leading to cultural wars and societal tension. Psychedelics can dissolve these stages and facilitate the advancement towards higher levels of consciousness.
  • Misuse and Demonization of Psychedelics: There are instances where psychedelics are misused, leading to bad trips and enhancing the stigma associated with these substances. However, such 'bad trips' can also be positive indicators of personal growth. This demonization is not a new phenomenon as history presents numerous cases of persecution for psychedelic use in spiritual practices.
  • The Battle of Exoteric and Esoteric Beliefs: In the wider context, societal issues such as addiction, mental health problems, and violence can be attributed to an ongoing battle between exoteric (materialistic) and esoteric (spiritual) beliefs. The suppression of esoteric beliefs through commercialization and materialism leads to a disconnection from existential truths.
  • Legalization and Research of Psychedelics: The legalization of certain psychedelics like LSD and mushrooms is proposed, taking into account their minimal risks compared to other legal substances. Government funding for research into these substances is also advocated to unlock their full potential for science and medicine.
  • Education and Psychedelic Licensing Program: A high-quality education campaign grounded in truth, science, and research is needed to counter negative propaganda about psychedelics. The concept of a psychedelic licensing program, involving an online course, exam, and observed psychedelic use in a safe environment, is suggested as the ultimate solution.
  • Future Perspective of Psychedelics: In the long-term view, significant advancements in the field of psychedelics are predicted, contributing to a major societal evolution. This requires a balance between responsible use and personal development, and maintaining an openness to change for progress. Larger societal shifts, like the adoption of self-driving cars, are paralleled with the predicted normalization of psychedelic use in the future.
  • Psychedelic Licensing and Pharmacy: Leo Gura suggests a licensing system where individuals can take an online course, pass an exam, and get a license to purchase psychedelics such as LSD, mushrooms, mescaline, DMT, and 5-MeO-DMT from a pharmacy. This approach allows for responsible use and helps in educating those who misuse these substances.
  • The Future of Psychedelics: Gura anticipates significant advancements in psychedelics research in the next 50-100 years. He posits that the resulting scientific developments and insights could lead to a major societal evolution and a potential renaissance in various fields, including science, technology, and arts.
  • Psychedelics and Personal Education: Gura stresses that while psychedelics can serve as a catalyst, they should be used in combination with consciousness work, personal development, and ongoing education.
  • Balancing use of Psychedelics: Gura highlights the need for a balanced approach to using psychedelics. He criticizes both the excesses of the 1960s and the later restrictive responses, suggesting the need to find a middle ground for effective use.
  • Role of Psychedelics in Consciousness Evolution: Gura observes that psychedelics are essential to evolving conscious thinking, radically changing perspectives, and addressing societal issues.
  • Psychedelic Experiences in Education: Gura imagines a future where psychedelics feature as part of the education system. He likens the future use of psychedelics to current developments like self-driving cars, proposing that people may not be considered fully-functioning members of society without experiencing psychedelics.
  • Psychedelic Use and Personal Effort: Gura emphasizes the importance of giving people the chance to responsibly use and better understand psychedelics. He acknowledges the existence of risks and dangers associated with their use and the importance of exploring different perspectives on the topic.
  • Addressing Psychedelic Myths and Misconceptions: Gura encourages individuals to try psychedelics responsibly under safe circumstances and with an open mind, this is to gain firsthand experience instead of relying on misconceptions and stereotypes.
