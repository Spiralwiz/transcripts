Making Sense Of Jordan Peterson - The Big Picture

  • Jordan Peterson's Influence and Criticisms: Jordan Peterson is a highly-regarded public intellectual who has a significant influence on young individuals due to his teachings on psychology, personal development, spirituality, and politics. However, his political views are subject to wide criticism, especially from progressives due to his conservative leanings.
  • Spiral Dynamics: This is essentially a psychological model emerging from developmental psychology that maps the development and evolution of the human psyche both individually and collectively. The model presents a predictable series of stages that a psyche undergoes, defined by one's values, which grow more complex at each stage.
  • Stages of Spiral Dynamics: Stages described include Stage Red (warlord mindset), Stage Blue (religious ideologues), Stage Orange (materialistic and scientific mindset). Each stage has healthy and unhealthy manifestations and plays a role in shaping one's perspective of the world.
  • Stage Green: This stage represents a more progressive mindset focused on equality and social accountability.
  • Spiral Dynamics and Jordan Peterson: The framework of Spiral Dynamics is applied in understanding and analyzing Peterson's teachings, why they resonate with so many people, and to pinpoint the limitations of these teachings.
  • Peterson's Misunderstanding of Progressivism: Understandings extracted from Spiral Dynamics suggest that Peterson misunderstands the concept of Stage Green (progressivism) by associating it negatively with communism, leading to misconceptions about his own stance and progressive values.
  • Beyond Peterson's Teachings: While applauding Peterson's teachings on societal issues, it is suggested that a broader and deeper exploration of stages beyond his 'Stage Blue' (conservatively religious or traditional) approach is necessary to unlock limitless potential and pursuit of truth and spirituality.
  • Deconstructing Beliefs and Discovering Consciousness: Success and external achievements do not necessarily bring happiness or love; true comfort is found in surrendering one's life and discovering oneself as part of God's infinite love and consciousness.
  • Hindrances to Higher Levels of Consciousness: Fear, complacency, and laziness prevent people from reaching higher levels of consciousness and understanding. Life struggles require dedication over a long period to attain states of Gods consciousness and love.
  • Jordan Peterson's Misconception on Human Development: Peterson's teachings tend to perpetuate a relativistic mindset, not exploring the idea of mankind's interconnected divinity and resulting in his remaining misunderstood and even unexplored by followers. Fear is highlighted as a factor that keeps individuals from realizing their full potential and achieving enlightenment.
  • Necessity for Transcending Stages: The lower stages, while necessary, do not suffice for solving systemic problems or societal dysfunctions that stem from human ego, selfishness, and fear. Advancing through various stages of human development, attention turns to expanded consciousness beyond psychology.
  • Understanding Spiral Dynamics stages: Spiral Dynamics is a model that encapsulates the development of human values and worldviews. The stages include stage Orange (success-driven), stage Blue (devotion to order and loyalty to traditional social norms), and stage Green (great emphasis on equality and social fairness). Each stage isn't innately "good" or "bad", they just represent different worldviews and can mutate into unhealthy versions if pushed to extremes.
  • The Role of Jordan Peterson: Intellectual Jordan Peterson noticed that many people, particularly young individuals in academia, are heavily influenced by the values of stage Green. These people, however, lack grounding in the values of stage Orange and Blue, creating an imbalance. Petersons work serves as a reminder not to abandon traditional stage Blue values like discipline and work ethic.
  • The Cultural War Between Stage Orange and Green: In many developed Western societies, a cultural conflict arises between individuals leaning towards stage Orange and those favoring stage Green. This leads to misunderstandings and conflicts since each stage has a radically different worldview.
  • Stage blue and its importance: While some aspects of Stage Blue (religious ideology, strong duty-consciousness) may be seen as outdated, Peterson emphasizes the importance of discipline, responsibility, and work ethic that can be learned from this stage. Issues arise when people dismiss or neglect these values, causing personal and societal dysfunctions.
  • Concept of Shadows in Stages: Every stage has a "shadow," essentially the tendency to demonize and dismiss the preceding stage. For example, stage Green often demonizes stage Blue as regressive, whereas stage Blue sees stage Green as possibly insane due to their contrasting worldviews.
  • Shallow and Hollow Pursuits of Success: Stage Orange can sometimes evolve into a toxic stage, characterized by predatory capitalism and society's pursuit of money and success. This pursuit often leaves people feeling empty and unfulfilled, thus the need to progress to Stage Green.
  • Introduction to Stage Green: Stage Green embraces pluralism and places a high value on fairness, equality, and communal well-being. This stage's values often conflict with those of stage Blue and Orange, which results in cultural warfare, particularly in developed Western countries.
  • Importance of Understanding Different Stages: Gura emphasizes the importance of understanding and integrating the different stages rather than demonizing or neglecting some. This comprehensive view helps to reduce misunderstandings and conflict within society.
  • Petersons Problem with Stage Green: Jordan Peterson often criticizes Stage Green (the stage associated with social justice warriors and Progressives). He believes that those aligned with Stage Green have neglected important lessons from stage Blue and Orange, which could result in personal difficulties and societal issues. His role is reminding people of these neglected traditional values.
  • Critique of Modern Society: Leo Gura criticizes modern society for leaning too strongly towards Stage Orange, leading to a culture obsessed with material success, resulting in a shallow existence. He suggests that more integration of the stage green attitude could lead to a better balance, creating a society that is more empathetic and compassionate.
  • Jordan Peterson's reconnection with Stage Blue principles: Jordan Peterson successfully embodies healthy Stage Blue principles of discipline and work ethic without falling into its negative aspects, such as intolerance or exclusivity. He emphasizes the importance of these principles in both personal lives and wider societies, arguing against complete dismissal of Stage Blue beliefs. Peterson's ethically-aligned Stage Blue values offer a framework to individuals feeling unfulfilled or lost in the Stage Orange or Green dominant culture. However, he also has a significant shadow of Stage Green, causing a limited understanding and negative view of progressivism.

Traditional religions and ideologies were heavily present in societal history leading to severe consequences like holy wars, genocides, and oppression.
