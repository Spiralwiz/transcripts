Lifestyle Minimalism - Renouncing Your Busy Stupid Life

  • Concept of Lifestyle Minimalism: Leo discusses lifestyle minimalism as a way to double one's life's happiness by significantly slowing down life's pace. This is not just about doing fewer activities but is more about doing the remaining activities slower and more mindfully. He suggests a drastic reduction of activities and commitments by approximately 25%-50% and doing the remaining activities 50% slower. This change is strategic and intended for the long term, ultimately encompassing the entirety of one's lifestyle.
  • Distinction between Being and Doing: Leo highlights the distinction between "being" and "doing", with our modern, fast-paced lives being overly centered on "doing". He advocates for a stronger focus on "being", asserting its importance for our self-actualization journey.
  • Efficiency and Quality of Life: Leo argues that our lives' pace significantly affects the quality of our life, which most people don't consider. The hectic speed of our customary lifestyles is considered totally unacceptable as we lose time for contemplation, introspection, awareness, consciousness, and strategic thinking.
  • Implementing Lifestyle Minimalism: Leo presents this form of minimalism as a high-level structural change to our lives. To achieve this, he suggests slowing down life's pace strategically and continuously, giving examples such as taking longer to mindfully eat a meal. The principle applies to all activities in life.
  • Minimalism vs. Material Minimalism: Transitioning from the concept of material minimalism (decluttering physical objects from life), Leo introduces the idea of lifestyle minimalism, which involves decluttering activities rather than objects. His view is that the latter is more important as the current western lifestyle leaves limited or no free time.
  • Living a Simple Life: Advocating for a simple life, Leo refers to the ancient Roman cultural ideal of a Spartan lifestyle. He suggests that the main benefit of living a simpler life with fewer activities is the resultant increased free time for thinking and contemplation, a form of 'wealth' that they consider more valuable than money.
  • Modern Western Lifestyle's Drawbacks: Leo argues that the modern western lifestyle is not conducive to self-actualization, primarily due to its unhealthy and neurotic nature. He attributes this to societal structures and advancing technology.
  • Virtue of Free Time: Leo argues that although the pursuit of success and money is typically seen as the path to fulfillment, the correlation between free time for contemplation and awareness is much higher than success and fulfillment. This contemplation time becomes valuable as it contributes to increasing our life's levels of fulfillment.
  • Implementing Lifestyle Changes: Emphasizing that self-actualization work involves "being" more than "doing", Leo offers that achieving a more fulfilling life may require strategic long-term changes to strip life back to its bare essentials and slow down its pace. As a consequence of this shift, one may discover much higher fulfillment in life.
  • His Own Lifestyle: Leo shares his own lifestyle to illustrate the effectiveness of lifestyle minimalism. He mentions that while his lifestyle may come across as boring to others, he finds it highly fulfilling because his simple lifestyle provides him with ample time for reflection, contemplation, and being. This, he emphasizes, contributes significantly to his emotional health.
  • On Overcommitment and Life's Fast Pace: Leo argues that many people try to do too much too fast, which prevents them from being mindful and aware of life. He suggests the fast pace people adopt can stem from a desire to avoid the discomfort of becoming self-aware and conscious.
  • Experience of a Meditation Retreat: Leo shares his experience with meditation retreats where participants are forbidden from speaking or using devices for 10 days. He describes the initial withdrawal symptoms during the early days of the retreat as challenging; however, these eventually give way to a deep sense of fulfillment and happiness.
  • Return to Fast-Paced Everyday Life After Retreat: Post-retreat, participants experience the stark contrast between the slow, mindful pace of the retreat and the hectic, fast-paced "normal" life as they return to their everyday routines and engagements.
  • The Concept of Being More Than Doing: Leo encourages the audience to contemplate a life that resembles the peaceful and fulfilling pace of the meditation retreat rather than their current fast-paced, activity-filled lifestyle.
  • Visualizing a Slowed-down Life: Leo helps his audience undertake a visualization exercise to imagine what their lives would look like and feel like if they slowed down by 50%. He believes this imagining could lead to significant improvements in mood and overall fulfillment.
  • Breaking Ties and Decluttering Life: For a meaningful life, Leo suggests strategically decluttering life by consciously pulling out of certain activities, commitments, hobbies, and relationships that don't contribute to one's fulfillment. This also includes continuously saying 'no' to additional commitments and distractions that may arise in the future.
  • Renouncing Material Life for Self-Actualization: Leo highlights that material success does not equate to personal fulfillment. He emphasizes the need to make a long-range strategic decision to renounce material life altogether for authentic self-actualization. This strategy involves extracting oneself from materially-oriented life patterns, which can include stepping out of certain relationships, commitments, hobbies, and recreational activities.
  • Perceived Loss of Excitement and Fun: Upon adopting this lifestyle change, Leo warns that one may be perceived as leading a dull life by friends or society. However, he argues that giving up on the conventional definition of "having a life," full of materialistic pursuits and social obligations, ironically allows one to gain "true life." He equates the typical bustling life to a gerbil running in a wheel or living like a domesticated cow, implying they are not experiencing real life.
  • Challenging Preconceived Attitudes: He asks viewers to challenge their perceived notions regarding the fulfilment of successful or hyperactive individuals. Leo asserts that such persons are often not fulfilled and that their hyperactivity is a result of a lack of inner fulfillment. He encourages viewers to examine the lives of these individuals closely and cautions against accepting their claims of happiness at face value.
  • Material Success and Fulfillment: He highlights instances where successful individuals who have built considerable wealth over time come to a realization that their achievements have not increased their sense of fulfillment. Leo acknowledges such self-discovery could be uncomfortable and many choose to ignore or deny it.
  • Proposition on Life Focus: Leo proposes a life strategy that involves creating space to focus on a few key areas: meditation, enlightenment, life purpose, learning/education, deliberate mindful action, contemplation, and being while ruthlessly minimizing distractions including relationships, family commitments, hobbies, and recreational activities. This strategy, he argues, if practiced consistently can lead to immense satisfaction and an extraordinary life.
  • Impact of Alienation from Material Life: He acknowledges that detaching from the materialistic aspects of life can lead to guilt or fear but emphasizes the long-term benefits of this strategic shift. By focusing on the chosen areas, Leo believes oneÂ’s life can be transformative and rewarding.
  • Long Term Consequences of Materialistic Lifestyle: If this strategy is ignored and one continues to live a materialistic life, dabbling in hobbies, relationships, work, entertainments etc., Leo suggests such a lifestyle would lead to an average life, potentially filled with regrets and unfulfilled dreams.
  • Focus on self and cultivation of being: To find true satisfaction in life, you must turn inward, focusing on your inner growth and being. Without this focus, you will feel unfulfilled, like life has let you down, and lack motivation to do anything significant. This may even lead to depression and anxiety due to strategic thinking failures. Leo shares that his life is simple, with limited activities, entertainment, and social engagements. While his life might look below average from the outside, it is deeply fulfilling. However, he still deviates from his strategy occasionally, emphasizing the importance of constant recommitment.
  • Monastic lifestyle & meditation: Leo's lifestyle is becoming more like that of a monk or sage, with increased periods for reading and meditation. Devoting an extra hour to meditation per day yields incredibly amazing feelings, comparable to that of a primal childhood state of pure being. He advocates for creating a lifestyle resembling a constant summer vacation, offering relaxation and deep connection to life.
  • Long-term strategy for life: Living a more fulfilling life is not a quick fix but a long-term strategy. It requires letting go of success and goal-oriented thinking and frequently declining new distractions. Achieving this lifestyle also entails the cultivation of a spiritual connection to reality, bringing you to a more conscious existential awareness.
  • Coping with carnal cravings: Overcoming cravings for food, sex, drugs, fun, and stimulations of all sorts is a part of the strategy. As long as you are consumed by these cravings, you cannot execute the strategy of lifestyle minimalism effectively. The way to overcome addiction, according to Leo, is through sitting and doing nothing.
  • Practical implementation of ideas: Leo urges viewers to execute the lifestyle minimizing strategies and observe their impact firsthand after a few years.
