AL-LAD Trip Report - A Powerful Tool For Consciousness Work

  • Using chemical substances for consciousness work instead of pleasure: Leo Gura emphasizes the use of psychoactive substances not for pleasure or recreational purposes, but for consciousness expansion, personal development, and gaining a better understanding of reality. He references studying the works of Carlos Castaneda and experimenting with psychedelics himself.
  • Differences between AL-LAD and other psychedelics: AL-LAD has been described as longer-lasting, mellow, and easy to handle compared to other substances like 5-MeO-DMT and mushrooms. It is not as fast-acting as 5-MeO-DMT and is less distorted than mushrooms, making AL-LAD a valuable tool for personal development and boosting everyday consciousness.
  • Leo's experiences with AL-LAD: Leo Gura shares insights from his experience with AL-LAD. After preparing through meditation, setting intentions, and visualizing, he consumes the substance. Leo emphasizes that this serious intent for personal growth differentiates his experience from that of recreational users.
  • Comparing AL-LAD to other substances: AL-LAD is an analog of LSD, with similar characteristics but notable differences. It has a shorter duration, is more visual but less mentally disturbing, and is often considered a party drug. However, Leo experiences it as more profound than its party drug reputation suggests.
  • Pre-trip preparation technique: To combat pre-trip anxiety, Leo introduces a technique of dancing and listening to fun music for about 20 minutes before the psychoactive substance takes effect. This has been found to boost mood and minimize initial concerns and negative thoughts.
  • Effects of AL-LAD: Leo describes the effects of AL-LAD, noting enhanced tactile sensations and a feeling of a wonderful body high. He clarifies that when he uses the term ecstasy, he is referring to the feeling of ecstasy, not the drug itself.
  • AL-LAD's impact on personal development: Leo views AL-LAD as an instrument for personal development. It has helped him shift from a logistics-based mindset, concerned with future tasks, to a state of being present and connected. The state of being present, according to Leo, has immense value and transcends mundane day-to-day life.
  • Psychedelic substances and creativity: Leo explains that psychedelic substances, like AL-LAD, produce an experience of heightened creativity, helping to stimulate exceptional ideas. However, he emphasizes that these experiences are difficult to understand without personal exposure.
  • Using AL-LAD as a beginner psychedelic: Leo recommends LSD as a beginner psychedelic due to its mild and mellow effects. AL-LAD is suggested as a companion psychedelic after a significant breakthrough. It is important to integrate and not use these substances more than once a week. Meditation, journaling, contemplation, and visualization are encouraged before tripping to maximize the transformative potential of psychedelic experiences.
  • The importance of intention in using psychedelics: Leo emphasizes how seriousness and intention when using psychedelics for metaphysical truths, healing, or personal growth can shape the experience. He further underscores the value of mindfulness practice as the foundation for advanced work with psychedelics.
  • Effects of psychedelics on the understanding of reality: Through using psychedelics, Leo discusses the realization that our personal lives and models of reality are constructs. This perspective can reveal the larger context of 'nothingness' and show that our current realities are limited. Achieving this realization requires a profound conscious breakthrough, made possible through the use of psychedelics.
  • Experience of ego death: Leo discusses his experience of ego death achieved through a relatively small dose of a psychedelic drug. He describes ego death as a sense of expanding and becoming a space permeated by conscious nothingness.
  • Fear of insanity during psychedelic experience: Leo expresses that the fear of insanity can prevent people from fully experiencing altered states of consciousness. He shares that enlightenment involves losing all sense of conventional reality and that insanity is a social concept.
  • Enhancing consciousness through psychedelics: Psychedelics, according to Leo, can provide profound experiences that expand consciousness. They reveal spiritual impurities and contribute to personal development. Patterns and constructs are shattered during non-symbolic experiences, offering glimpses of infinity that may be challenging but rewarding.
  • The effects of psychedelic substances on mind: Leo explains that the use of psychedelic substances has a transformative impact. Despite his analytical work that often leads to a state of constant thought, Leo feels a deep sense of peace after a psychedelic trip. Psychedelics help him to overcome fears and inspire him to let go of the need to plan and control life.
  • The impact of psychedelics on personal values and life purposes: Deep realizations during psychedelic trips lead Leo to lesser desire for consensus reality and less need to please people. His core values shifted towards consciousness work rather than materialistic achievement. The use of psychedelic substances also led Leo to view meditation as a way to alter the structure of reality, with his body energy improving post-trip.
  • Handling Anxiety in Psychedelic Experiences: Leo suggests that pre-trip anxiety can lead to unpleasant experiences with psychedelic substances as the substances amplify the user's current emotional state. To counteract this, he recommends engaging in fun, physical activities like dancing to fun music for about 20 minutes before the effects start. This helps in boosting the user's mood and creates a more pleasant state for the psychedelic experience.
  • Physical Effects of AL-LAD: About 45 minutes into taking AL-LAD, Leo starts feeling an amazing body high, experiencing enhanced tactile sensations. For him, it feels great to be present in his body, every touch feels 'delicious' and luxurious, also experiencing a form of mild ecstasy. Being in this physical state makes him feel like his body is finally functioning as it should be.
  • Shifting From Logistical to Present Reality: AL-LAD allows Leo to shift from a logistical mindset, which is constantly planning ahead and worrying about future tasks, to a state of intense presence and being. It gives him a preview of what he is working towards with his meditation practices.
  • Experience of Being: AL-LAD connects Leo with being, a state of intense presence and awareness. He describes this state as worth more than mundane daily challenges or accomplishments. It's a state difficult to explain and one needs to experience it to understand this.
  • Direct Conscious Experience of Altered Thought Stream: While under the influence of AL-LAD, Leo observes his usually scattered thought stream becoming slow, steady, and focused, somewhat like 'a laser beam.' His mind becomes 'hyper-connected,' 'super-integrated,' and 'super intuitive,' enabling him to deeply understand various topics.
  • Enhanced Creativity with AL-LAD: AL-LAD enhances creativity to a 'genius level,' where brilliant ideas come effortlessly. Creativity becomes easy and Leo compares this state to coming in contact with a divine capacity—-the muse—-fueling the creative work. However, he notes that the ideas generated during the trip may not retain their original brilliance when reviewed post-trip due to state-dependent memory and creativity.
  • Physical Sensation of Brain Fuzziness with AL-LAD: Leo experiences a sensation similar to a head cold, which he describes as 'brain fuzziness.' Although it might seem uncomfortable, the overall atmosphere is pleasant and enjoyable due to the enhanced physical sensations created by AL-LAD.
  • Shamanic use of Psychedelics vs Recreational use: Leo reiterates his interest in the shamanic use of psychedelics, which involves using these substances to transform ones understanding of reality and consciousness. He warns against using psychedelics for leisure activities, emphasizing the importance of having a genuine intention to grow ones consciousness when using these substances.
  • Chemical Substances and Physical Sensations: Leo Gura describes the physical sensations he experienced during his psychedelic trip, such as a body high akin to ecstasy. He talks about his thoughts transforming while remaining conscious, which he compares to a childlike state of mind. He discusses some drawbacks, such as a fuzzy feeling in the head, but argues they are comparatively minor.
  • Visual Effects of AL-LAD: AL-LAD is reported to have strong visuals, but Leo says he experienced very few, less than he would on mushrooms. Despite this, he didn't view it as an issue due to the other effects.
  • Positive and Mellow Psychedelic: Leo classifies AL-LAD as a very mellow and smooth psychedelic. It induces no discomfort or nausea and leads to overall positivity. He suggests that for a first-time psychedelic experience, choosing a substance that results in positivity would be better than an intense or disruptive trip.
  • The Value of Aesthetic Beauty and Creativity: During the trip, Leo experienced enhanced appreciation for mundane things, which took on a new, beautiful aspect. He also felt a strong connection to creativity, as if he were immersing himself in the presence of an artistic muse. This sensation was significant since he values creativity deeply.
  • Entering Deep Spiritual Work: Expecting a light-hearted trip, Leo was surprised to dive into profound spiritual work. Many years of knowledge and insight became real and tangible. Despite anticipating a casual experience, he had profound insights that reshaped his perception thoroughly.

He was deeply affected by the overwhelming beauty of the moment, prompting him to adjust his life's priorities. He also recognised the ineffable nature of being and surrendered to his inability to verbalise or symbolise it.

Everything known is viewed through the lens of our own personal context, which restricts perception to the extent that it can fail to recognise potential insights. Recognising this limiting context is key to gaining a deeper understanding of oneself and one's place in the universe.
  • Importance of Perspective: The experience reveals a larger context than the ones we customarily perceive, a context of nothingness. This perspective exposes how limiting our usual reality is and how it only makes sense when we ignore the larger context. This revelation is extremely profound, and grasping it fundamentally alters perception.

Questioning this context is often met with disbelief, but Psychedelic experiences reveal a new context, showing us our standard view of reality is a construct, a mere facet of reality, limited and making sense only when the larger context is ignored.
